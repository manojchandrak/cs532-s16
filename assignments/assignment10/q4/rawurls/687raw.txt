<p class="leading">Samir Nasri has hailed Manchester City's progression and is eyeing a long-term stay at the Etihad Stadium.</p>
<div class="module module-ad fresh8-inline-leaderboar">
<!-- Begin: Fresh8 placement --> <div id="f8-005"></div><!-- End: Fresh8 placement --></div>
<!-- Article Start -->
<a href="http://www.goal.com/en-gb/teams/england/manchester-city/676?ICID=HP_TN_4_2_9">Manchester City</a> winger <a href="http://www.goal.com/en-gb/people/france/7626/samir-nasri?ICID=TP_TST_24">Samir Nasri</a> has revealed he would love to finish his career with the Premier League club.<br />
<br />
The 28-year-old, who is closing in on a return from a serious thigh injury which has kept him out of action since October, signed from Arsenal in a deal reportedly worth &pound;25 million in August 2011.<br />
<br />
Nasri has been impressed by how City have continued to develop since his arrival and would be keen on a long-term stay with a team he thinks are on track to become the world&#39;s biggest.<br />
<br />
&quot;This club is doing everything to be the most dominant in the world,&quot; he told the club&#39;s official website.<br />
<br />
&quot;We have an ambitious owner who tries to improve the team and facilities every single year.<br />
<br />
&quot;I have a great relationship with the chairman. It is not just City, with all the clubs around the world it is like one big family.&nbsp;There are so many exciting things happening here.<br />
<br />
&quot;You never know what will happen in football, but it would be a pleasure to finish my career at City.&quot;<br />
