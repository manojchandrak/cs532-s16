                        <div class="trb_ar_page"  data-role="pagination_page" data-content-page="1">
                          <p>A nurse charged with sexually assaulting a patient while he was working at the Bowie Health Center will go on trial Thursday in Upper Marlboro.</p><p>Jared Nathan Kline of Springfield, <a title="Washington, D.C." href="/topic/us/washington-d.c.-PLGEO1001012-topic.html">Virginia</a>, faces charges of second-degree assault and fourth-degree sex offense following an incident in January 2014.</p><div class="trb_gptAd trb_ar_main_ad" data-role="adloader adloader_ad delayload delayload_done" data-state="adloader_unloaded"  data-withinviewport-options="bottomOffset=100&topOffset=1000000"  data-adloader-position="articleCube" data-adloader-size="300x250" data-adloader-adtype="articlebottomcube"  ></div
    >
  <p>A woman told police she came into the emergency room at the Bowie Health Center feeling ill; Kline was the nurse attending to her, the Prince George's County States Attorney's Office said in a statement released Wednesday.</p><p>Kline entered the hospital room to draw blood from the victim and while checking her outstretched arm for a vein, Klineallegedly placed his groin into the victim's open hand, prosecutors said in describing the charges. Kline then left the room and returned to administer the IV and medicine and while administering the IV, he allegedly placed his genitals in the woman's hand.</p><span class="trb_ar_cont" data-ar-cont="Article continues below"></span>
