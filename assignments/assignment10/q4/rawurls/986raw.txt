<div id="WNStoryBody" class=" withRelated right"><p>LOUISVILLE, Ky. (WDRB) -- With a constant demand for addiction treatment services, the Healing Place will open a new recovery center next week.&nbsp;</p><p>Recovery on Chestnut&nbsp;sits at the corner of W. Chestnut and Dr. WJ&nbsp;Hodge Streets in Louisville.&nbsp;The program will include a short stay, followed by long&nbsp;term monitoring.&nbsp;</p><p>&quot;We think we're gonna fill a large need in this community,&quot; Recovery on Chestnut Administrator Pat Fogarty said about the facility.&nbsp;</p><p>Fogarty says he'd like to reach the middle class.&nbsp;</p><p>It will be a recovery center for those&nbsp;who don't need to go to the Healing Place, which is free and often used as a last resort, but&nbsp;can't pay for expensive, out-of-state rehabs.&nbsp;</p><p>&quot;We want to be at a price point that the middle class can afford that you can put on a credit card or you can use third party financing.&quot; Forgarty told WDRB News.&nbsp;</p><p>The 16-bed program will cost $5,000&nbsp;and it's open to men 18-years-old and up.&nbsp;</p><p>Fogarty says the organization expects to help people addicted to different kinds of drugs,&nbsp;including heroin.&nbsp;</p><p>&quot;In my opinion and my experience, heroin is very much a middle class drug. It's very much&nbsp;an&nbsp;East End drug,&quot; he said.&nbsp;</p><p>Forgarty says the heroin epidemic keeps getting worse in Louisville.&nbsp;</p><p>The Healing Place is forced to turn hundreds of people away each month because they don't have enough beds.&nbsp;But funds from this new facility&nbsp;will go toward helping others.&nbsp;</p><p>&quot;We're trying to be proactive as a non-profit and any revenue that we generate will fund the Healing Place mission,&quot; Fogarty explained.&nbsp;</p><p>The Healing Place is working to build and add more beds.&nbsp;</p><p>Fogarty says they'd also like to open up a facility like Recovery on Chestnut for women.&nbsp;</p><p><em>Copyright 2016 WDRB Media.
