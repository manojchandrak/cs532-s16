<p class="leading">Patricio Toranzo and Diego Mendoza both sustained injuries as Huracan's team bus overturned following the club's Copa Libertadores fixture against Caracas</p>
<div class="module module-ad fresh8-inline-leaderboar">
<!-- Begin: Fresh8 placement --> <div id="f8-005"></div><!-- End: Fresh8 placement --></div>
<!-- Article Start -->
<p>Ex-Argentina international Patricio Toranzo is expected to play again despite having multiple partial toe amputations after <a href="http://www.goal.com/en/teams/argentina/hurac%C3%A1n/123">Huracan</a>'s coach overturned following a Copa Libertadores match in Venezuela.</p>
<p>Both Toranzo and Diego Mendoza, who scored a stoppage-time goal to send Huracan into the group stage, were treated after the crash as the squad travelled to the airport the day after their match against Caracas.<br /><br />Fitness coach Pablo Santella is also reported to have sustained a minor injury.</p>
<p>"Toranzo had four of the toes on his left foot partially amputated," a source confirmed to <strong>Goal Argentina</strong>.</p>
<p><img style="border: 1px solid black;" src="http://u.goal.com/2669000/2669022.jpg" alt="" width="100%" /></p>
<p><img style="border: 1px solid black;" src="http://u.goal.com/2668900/2668992.jpg" alt="" width="100%" /></p>
<p>"He can still play, his big toe is OK which is where he lays most of the weight of his body. We have to wait until the wound heals. That will take between three of four weeks, his toes may be shorter!"</p>
<p>Brake problems are said to be behind the incident in Venezuela, although no official announcement has been made on the cause.</p>
<p>"The most important thing to say is that all [the] players are good," club president Alejandro Nadur explained. "It was scary, but they are all good."</p>
