      <p>Two longtime Harford County sheriff's deputies were shot to death in broad daylight Wednesday at a busy shopping center by a man officials believe was targeting police, according to authorities.</p><p>The 68-year-old suspect, whom officials described as a vagrant, was also killed in the confrontation in Abingdon, a closely knit community 30 miles northeast of Baltimore.</p><div class="trb_gptAd trb_ar_main_ad" data-role="adloader adloader_ad delayload delayload_done" data-state="adloader_unloaded"  data-withinviewport-options="bottomOffset=100&topOffset=1000000"  data-adloader-position="articleCube" data-adloader-size="300x250" data-adloader-adtype="articlebottomcube"  ></div
    >
  <p>The mayhem erupted at a Panera Bread restaurant shortly before noon in the Boulevard at Box Hill shopping center. </p><p><i><b>&nbsp;<a href="http://www.baltimoresun.com/news/maryland/harford/abingdon/bs-md-harford-shooting-thursday-20160211-story.html" target="_blank">&raquo;&raquo; Read Thursday's updates here.</a></b></i></p><span class="trb_ar_cont" data-ar-cont="Article continues below"></span>

Sheriff Jeffrey R. Gahler said there were two warrants for Evans' arrest &#8212; a criminal warrant for allegedly assaulting a police officer in Florida and a civil warrant issued in Harford County.</p><p>Gahler said he believed Evans targeted one of the deputies inside the Panera &quot;because he was in a police uniform.&quot;</p><p>The deputies are believed to be the first in Harford to be killed by gunfire on duty in more than a century.</p><p>The incident Wednesday was the first time in almost 15 years that two Maryland law enforcement officers were killed in a single shooting.</p><p>Two Prince George's County sheriff's deputies were shot and killed in a 2002 incident. Two officers were shot to death in 2001 in a trailer park in Centreville on the Eastern Shore.</p><p>In Abingdon on Wednesday, deputies scoured multiple crime scenes for evidence hours after the shooting.</p><p>The incident took about 15 minutes, officials said. It began when a deputy was shot in the head inside the restaurant in the 3400 block of Merchant Blvd. about 11:45 a.m., officials said.</p><span class="trb_ar_cont" data-ar-cont="Article continues below"></span>

  </aside><p>Gahler said the deputy was responding to a call from the restaurant. He said he was unsure of the nature of the call.</p><p>After shooting the deputy, the gunman fled the restaurant, officials said. Witnesses pointed other deputies toward the Park View apartments, a senior living complex next to the shopping center on Box Hill South Parkway.</p><p>A deputy found the suspect outside the complex, officials said. Both the suspect and a deputy were shot.</p><p>One of the wounded deputies was taken to University of Maryland Upper Chesapeake Medical Center in Bel Air. The other was flown to the Maryland Shock Trauma Center in Baltimore.</p><p>Gahler said he met with the families of both.</p><p>&quot;Obviously, I don't think I have to tell anybody, there are no words to describe what they're going through right now,&quot; he said.</p><p>He said he knew the deputies well, and called them &quot;outstanding&quot; law enforcement officers.</p><p>Gahler said two other officers fired weapons at the scene. Both have been placed on administrative leave.</p><p>He said the investigation remains in the preliminary stages.</p><p>&quot;We fully suspect that both deputies were shot by the same suspect,&quot; Gahler said. &quot;We are not looking at anyone else. We believe it's an individual who's no longer a threat to the community.&quot;</p><p>No customers inside the Panera were injured.</p><p>Evans had an open criminal case in Florida for an alleged reckless-driving incident, according to online court records. The incident was alleged to have occurred last April. Glassman said. &quot;Whether you're serving a summons or warrant, a guy like that, who has a pistol on his body in a public place and is willing to go outside and shoot an officer, is a risk to all us.&quot;</p><p>Glassman said the two deputies showed &quot;selfless dedication.&quot; He ordered Harford County flags to be lowered to half-staff.</p><p>&quot;We are grateful that these deputies did give the ultimate sacrifice for all of us,&quot; he said. &quot;We also want to celebrate, with their families, their lives and their dedication to service.&quot;</p><aside class="trb_embed" data-content-id="85861978" data-content-size="small" data-content-type="story" data-content-slug="bal-map-two-harford-sheriff-s-deputies-killed-in-abingdon-20160210" data-content-subtype="htmlstory" data-role="socialshare_item  imgsize_ratiosizecontainer " data-state="">        <div class="trb_embed_media">        <span data-content-kicker="Map:"></span><a class="trb_embed_media_link"  href="/bal-map-two-harford-sheriff-s-deputies-killed-in-abingdon-20160210-htmlstory.html" data-content-media-present="true"><figure data-role=" delayload  delayload_done imgsize_item" class="trb_embed_imageContainer_figure"><img itemprop="image" data-baseurl="http://www.trbimg.com/img-56bbbd96/turbine/bal-map-two-harford-sheriff-s-deputies-killed-in-abingdon-20160210" alt="Two Harford sheriff&apos;s deputies killed in Abingdon" class="trb_embed_imageContainer_img" title="Two Harford sheriff&apos;s deputies killed in Abingdon" data-c-nd="1600x899" /></figure></a><div class="trb_embed_related" data-role="lightbox_metadata" >                      <a class="trb_embed_media_link" href="/bal-map-two-harford-sheriff-s-deputies-killed-in-abingdon-20160210-htmlstory.html"><span class="trb_embed_related_title">Two Harford sheriff's deputies killed in Abingdon</span>        <div class="trb_embed_related_caption">Authorities say a man shot two Harford County sheriff’s officers Wednesday in and around a Panera Bread restaurant in Abingdon. Jesse Bane said he listened to the incident unfold on police radio.</p><p>The deaths will strike the department hard, he said, because law enforcement officers form tight bonds.</p><p>&quot;You become part of a family,&quot; said Bane, the current town administrator in Bel Air. &quot;That's what makes these things very difficult for law enforcement agencies to deal with.&quot;</p><p>&quot;We've lost two brothers,&quot; he said.</p><p>Law enforcement in Harford County has suffered a series of losses in recent years.</p><p>The sheriff's office, composed of roughly 300 sworn officers, suffered the back-to-back deaths in September 2012 of Cpl.
