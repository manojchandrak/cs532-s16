<h1 style="max-width: 420px; padding-left: 15px;">Zinc’s Immune Link</h1>
        <div class="posted-in" style="padding-left: 15px;">
            <p class="text-info"><em>Posted on Feb. 10, 2016, 6 a.m. in
                
                    <a href="/list/news/immune_system/"
                       class="preferred-category"
                       >Immune System</a>
                
                
                    <a href="/list/news/functional_foods_/">Functional Foods</a>
                
                    <a href="/list/news/minerals/">Minerals</a>
                </em></p>
<div class="article-blurb">
    <p class="text-info"><em>The mineral affects how the immune system responds to inflammation.</em></p>
</div>
        </div>

<div style="margin-left:10px;" class="pull-right hidden-xs">
<a class="abstract-img-link rgac" href="http://thumb7.shutterstock.com/display_pic_with_logo/57225/141336385/stock-photo-a-platter-of-fresh-raw-oysters-on-ice-at-an-outdoor-cafe-141336385.jpg">
<img class="abstract-main" src="/images/homefeature/150507_oysters2.jpg"
         alt="Zinc’s Immune Link"
         width="200"
         height="200"
         border="0"
    />
</a>

    
    <div class="shutterstock"><a href="http://thumb7.shutterstock.com/display_pic_with_logo/57225/141336385/stock-photo-a-platter-of-fresh-raw-oysters-on-ice-at-an-outdoor-cafe-141336385.jpg">Platter of oysters - image from Shutterstock</a></div>
    



</div>
<div style="margin-left:10px;" class="visible-xs-block">
<a class="abstract-img-link rgac" href="http://thumb7.shutterstock.com/display_pic_with_logo/57225/141336385/stock-photo-a-platter-of-fresh-raw-oysters-on-ice-at-an-outdoor-cafe-141336385.jpg">
<img class="abstract-main" src="/images/homefeature/150507_oysters2.jpg"
         alt="Zinc’s Immune Link"
         class="img-responsive"
         border="0"
    />
</a>

    
    <div class="shutterstock pull-left"><a href="http://thumb7.shutterstock.com/display_pic_with_logo/57225/141336385/stock-photo-a-platter-of-fresh-raw-oysters-on-ice-at-an-outdoor-cafe-141336385.jpg">Platter of oysters - image from Shutterstock</a></div>
    

<br/><br/><br/>
</div>

<div class="article-indent" style="padding-left: 15px;">
	<p>Zinc is a key component in over 300 enzymes utilized in the body, and the mineral is also an important structural component of many proteins, hormones, neuropeptides, and hormone receptors.&nbsp; Previous studies report that zinc deficiency can result in immune dysfunction.&nbsp;&nbsp; Emily Ho, from Oregon State University (Oregon, USA), and colleagues employed a mouse model to conduct experiments to assess the physiological effects of zinc deficiency.&nbsp; The team showed that reducing zinc caused improper immune cell activation and dysregulation of a cytokine IL-6, a protein that affects inflammation in the cell.&nbsp; The investigators also compared zinc levels in living mice, young and old. The older mice had low zinc levels that corresponded with increased chronic inflammation and decreased IL-6 methylation &ndash; an epigenetic mechanism that cells use to control gene expression. Observing that: &ldquo;Zinc deficiency induced inflammatory response in part by eliciting aberrant immune cell activation and altered promoter methylation,&rdquo; the study authors submit that: &ldquo;Our results suggested potential interactions between zinc status, epigenetics, and immune function, and how their dysregulation could contribute to chronic inflammation.&rdquo;</p>
</div>

	<h4>
		ANTI-AGING TIP OF THE DAY

Tip #192 -  Stay Connected
</div>

<div>

Researchers from the University of Chicago (Illinois, USA) report that social isolation may be detrimental to both mental and physical health.  The team analyzed data from the National Social Life, Health, and Aging Project, a nationwide US study involving 3,000 men and women, ages 57 to 85 years.  They arrived at three key findings regarding the relationships between health and different types of isolation:
<br><br>
&bull; The researchers found that the most socially connected older adults are three times as likely to report very good or excellent health compared to those who are least connected, regardless of whether they feel isolated.
<br><br>
&bull; The team found that older adults who feel least isolated are five times as likely to report very good or excellent health as those who feel most isolated, regardless of their actual level of social connectedness. 
<br><br>
&bull; They determined that social disconnectedness is not related to mental health unless it brings feelings of loneliness and isolation. 
<br><br>Separately, Rush University Medical Center (Illinois, USA) researchers studied 906 older men and women, testing their motor functions (including grip, pinch strength, balance, and walking) and surveying their social activity, for a period of 5 years.  Those study participants with less social activity were found to have a more rapid rate of motor function decline. Specifically, the team found that every one-point decrease in social activity corresponded to an increase in functional aging of 5 years, translating to a 40% higher risk of death and 65% higher risk of disability.
