    <h1 class="title details-title" itemprop="headline">Barrie non-profits out in the cold after crash</h1>
        <div class="details-intro-area clearfix">
            <div class="details-intro">
                
                "The car was fully embedded in the building"
            
            </div>
                <a href="#details-shares" class="details-bubble details-bubble-s sc" data-url="https://www.barrietoday.com/local-news/barrie-non-profits-out-in-the-cold-after-crash-196083">
                    <span class="sc-s">0</span>
                    <i class="nub1"></i><i class="nub2"></i>
                </a>
        </div>
    <div class="details-byline">
            <time datetime="2016-02-10T22:37:06.4670000Z" itemprop="datePublished">Feb 10, 2016 5:37 PM</time>
 by: <span itemprop="author">Sue Sgambati</span>
            <div class="updated theme-colour">Updated <time datetime="2016-02-10T23:07:00.2670000Z" itemprop="dateModified">Feb 10, 2016 6:07 PM</time></div>

    </div>
<div class="captioned details-file" id="file-0"><img alt="sari " class="caption-img" content="https://vmcdn.ca/f/files/barrietoday/images/police/collisions/sari.jpg" itemprop="image" src="https://vmcdn.ca/f/files/barrietoday/images/police/collisions/sari.jpg;w=630"><div class="caption">Sari Russell, executive director of Deaf Access Simcoe Muskoka, says the organization has been displaced after a car crashed into its offices.  Sue Sgambati/BarrieToday</div></div>    <div id="details-body" class="details-body clear clearfix" itemprop="articleBody">
        <p>Sari Russell of Deaf Access <span class="scayt-misspell-word" data-scayt-word="Simcoe" data-scayt-lang="en_CA">Simcoe</span> <span class="scayt-misspell-word" data-scayt-word="Muskoka" data-scayt-lang="en_CA">Muskoka</span> stands in front of the gaping hole that used to be her office wall.</p><p>Shortly after 10 p.m. Tuesday, a driver crashed through the Anne Street building which houses four non-profit agencies: Deaf Access <span class="scayt-misspell-word" data-scayt-word="Simcoe" data-scayt-lang="en_CA">Simcoe</span> <span class="scayt-misspell-word" data-scayt-word="Muskoka" data-scayt-lang="en_CA">Muskoka</span>, the <span class="scayt-misspell-word" data-scayt-word="CNIB" data-scayt-lang="en_CA">CNIB</span>, the Alzheimer Society and Catholic Family Services.</p><p>The 39-year old man&nbsp;had to be extricated by Barrie Firefighters and <span class="scayt-misspell-word" data-scayt-word="Simcoe" data-scayt-lang="en_CA">Simcoe</span> County Paramedics.</p><p>Barrie Police later said he&nbsp;had suffered a medical emergency before the crash.</p><p>"The car was fully embedded in the building," Russell said while considering herself lucky she wasn't sitting at her desk when it happened.</p><p>"All four agencies are out of the building today," added the executive director who believes their displacement could be longer term.</p><p>"I believe the other three will be able to re-enter tomorrow or Friday awaiting engineer's approval. But Deaf Access Services, we're not sure when our space will be ready for usage again."</p><p>Russell says the agency is going to need all the help it can to re-establish its headquarters.</p><p>In the short term, Deaf Access is urging its clients to call or <span class="scayt-misspell-word" data-scayt-word="email" data-scayt-lang="en_CA">email</span> at info-@deafaccess.ca</p><p>Russell hopes the organization can share some space with the other agencies in the building.</p><p>Deaf Access <span class="scayt-misspell-word" data-scayt-word="Simcoe" data-scayt-lang="en_CA">Simcoe</span> <span class="scayt-misspell-word" data-scayt-word="Muskoka" data-scayt-lang="en_CA">Muskoka</span> services include sign-language interpretation and note-taking services for deaf and hard of hearing people for all of <span class="scayt-misspell-word" data-scayt-word="Simcoe" data-scayt-lang="en_CA">Simcoe</span> County and <span class="scayt-misspell-word" data-scayt-word="Muskoka" data-scayt-lang="en_CA">Muskoka</span>.</p><p>Russell acknowledges the random crash is something she would never have imagined.</p><p>"This was not on the scope of my job description when I took on this position but all in stride," said Russell. "Most importantly no was inside the building at the time. Our thoughts are with the man."</p>
