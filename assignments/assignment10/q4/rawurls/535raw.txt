honor the Nevada journalist who wrote for the Reno Gazette-Journal for 22 years.</p><p>Clifton's family, friends and former co-workers crowded the council chambers Wednesday to recognize his work telling the stories of the state's characters, special events and places Nevadans hold dear.</p><p>Clifton's position was eliminated last month as part of a reduction in force at RGJ Media.</p><p>Schieve's proclamation, read by her sister Amanda Schieve Crawford, honored Clifton as a "a renown and respected journalist who has dedicated his entire career to covering Nevada and Nevada history."</p><p>Clifton, a native Nevadan born in Gabbs, graduated from the University of Nevada, Reno with a journalism degree and worked for the Gardnerville Record-Courier, Tahoe Bonanza and Tahoe World before landing at the Reno Gazette-Journal. He became a nationally recognized Reno Rodeo reporter and covered other special events such as Hot August Nights for the RGJ.</p><p>Councilman David Bobzien remarked that Clifton's work pushed him to be a better elected official.</p><p>"Most importantly, the value of his journalism in informing me about the stories, the conditions, the issues that individuals face, those are our constituents," Bobzien said.
