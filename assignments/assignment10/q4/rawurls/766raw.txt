	<h1 class="entry-title">Bicycle Queensland calling for urgent action on cycling safety with release of new bikeway plans</h1>			<p class="meta"><span class="fa fa-clock-o"></span> February 10th, 2016</p>
              
			<p><img src="/images/uploads/images/news/poster-for-web.jpg" alt="Stanley St Bikeway" width="700" height="500" class="no-float no-shadow" /></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.5">Bicycle Queensland is calling on all parties contesting the upcoming council elections to commit to saving lives by funding the construction of a major separated bikeway along one of Brisbane&rsquo;s busiest and most dangerous roads.</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.5">Plans were today unveiled for the Stanley Street bikeway that would stretch 1.2 kilometres from the Gabba to the Goodwill Bridge at South Bank.</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.5">The proposed bikeway would create a critical infrastructure link that would allow thousands of people to cycle safely in a part of inner-Brisbane that is used by 45,000 vehicles per day.</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.5">Commencing at the corner of Ipswich Road and Stanley St, the two-way route would provide bike riders with separation from vehicles all the way along the busy traffic corridor, linking with South Bank, the Kangaroo Point Bikeway and the Goodwill Bridge to the CBD.</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.5">The plan also includes expanded pedestrian zones, newly paved areas, sculpture opportunities, a public plaza, and trees and shelters for shade to enhance Brisbane&rsquo;s reputation as a new world city.</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.5">Bicycle Queensland CEO Ben Wilson urged Brisbane&rsquo;s Lord Mayoral candidates to throw their support behind the proposal and commit to the construction of the bikeway in their election promises.</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.5">&ldquo;This commitment is relatively low in cost and in traffic implications, and is a golden opportunity to make a part of Brisbane rideable for the thousands of people in the rapidly growing Woolloongabba area and inner-eastern suburbs,&rdquo; said Mr Wilson.</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.5">&ldquo;Separated bikeways make riding so much safer and have a proven record of getting people taking up cycling for transport, which relieves congestion.&rdquo;</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.5">&ldquo;This bikeway&rsquo;s value-for-money is sensational and critically important to the city.&rdquo;</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.5">&ldquo;The project will lift this busy Stanley Street corridor from an ugly, hostile and dangerous road into a pleasant place to live, dine, commute by bike and walk along by creating a new buffer- boulevard.&rdquo;</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.5">The plan will also address some of the key recommendations made by the coroner in December following an inquest into the death of bike rider Rebekka Meyer at the Stanley Street/Annerley Road intersection in 2014.</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.5">Coroner Christine Clements recommended that more dedicated, exclusive bikeways be planned and developed across Brisbane to prevent a similar tragedy occurring, particularly at this location.</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.5">Mr Wilson said that not only will this bikeway address that recommendation, it will also incorporate the recommendation to widen the pedestrian crossing across Stanley Street, giving riders a safe, alternative route into Annerley Road</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.5">&ldquo;It&rsquo;s definitely a hotspot area with people riding from the city to the University of Queensland, a key bike route that accommodates up to 1,000 bicycle riders every day,&rdquo; said Mr Wilson.</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.5">&ldquo;This plan improves the safety of bike riders at the intersection by providing segregation and a dedicated space with minimal disruption to general traffic or commerce.&rdquo;</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.5">The project is the central plank of Bicycle Queensland&rsquo;s plan to make Brisbane a city where bike riding is a normal and safe activity for all.</span></p>
<p><span style="line-height: 1.5">Other key requests ahead of the upcoming election include:</span></p>
<ul>
<li>An increase in council&rsquo;s future bikeway commitments from the current $30m p.a. to $40m p.a. (ie&nbsp; 25 cent raise per Brisbane resident per week to walking and cycling infrastructure)</li>
<li>A CBD planning process to allow north-south and east-west segregated bikeways through the inner city by 2018</li>
<li>Engage with innovative trials of new and world class designs to get more space for cycling on important roads</li>
<li>Update the Brisbane Bicycle Network Plan and set a firm target of near-completion of the Brisbane network by 2024</li>
</ul>
<p>To view or download the full concept designs of the Stanley St &#8216;Gabba to Goodwill&#8217; Bikeway, <a href="https://www.dropbox.com/sh/j3dgwn06wj607s0/AAD0buYQtINvos1w_RmKsRNZa?dl=0" rel="external">click here.</a></p>
<p>To read more about Bicycle Queensland&#8217;s election priorities, <a rel="external" href="http://www.bq.org.au/news/bicycle-queenslands-brisbane-city-council-election-priorities/">click here.</a></p>
