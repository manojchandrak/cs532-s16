        <p><b><a href="http://www.bet.com/topics/a/adele.html">Adele</a></b> was up to a challenge most singers would be stressed out by. Back in January, Adele did her best <b><a href="http://www.bet.com/topics/n/nicki-minaj.html">Nicki Minaj</a></b>, and the result ended up being both magic — and record-setting.</p>

<p>During a recorded car ride with<b> James Corden</b>, Adele does Minaj proud, covering the rapper’s “Monster” with a gusto that would put other karaoke enthusiasts (and even some rappers —yeah, we said it) to shame.</p>


<p>Minaj was among millions to find the clip hilarious, giving the powerhouse singer props on <a href="https://twitter.com/nickiminaj/status/687521370385743872" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">Twitter</a>, pointing out Adele even nailed her “attitude &amp; fingers to match.” </p>


<p>Fast-forward to almost a full month later and the verdict is in: the clip has <a href="http://www.ew.com/article/2016/02/10/adele-carpool-karaoke-youtube-records" target="_blank" rel="nofollow">set a new record</a> on YouTube for the most-watched late-night clip… ever.<br />
</p>

<p>The “Carpool Karaoke” segment on <i>The Late Late Show With James Corden </i>set a new record on YouTube with 67.5 million views since it was released on January 13, with its first five days online racking up 42 million. Dang. </p>

<p>Now that we know Adele can spit with the best of ‘em, maybe Minaj will recruit the singer for a feature in the future. We'd be here for that.</p>
