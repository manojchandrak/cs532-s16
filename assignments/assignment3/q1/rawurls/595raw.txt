<p>Parliament voted to keep MPs arrests a secret tonight, under new rules pushed through by Tory minister Chris Grayling.</p> <p>In a chilling move, debated for less than an hour, the <a href="http://mirror.co.uk/all-about/house-of-commons">House of Commons</a> decided the public have no right to know if their MP is arrested</p> <p>Just one MP voted against the new rules.</p> <p>Until now, the names of members placed under arrest was a matter of public record and was announced to the House by Speaker <a href="http://mirror.co.uk/all-about/john-bercorw">John Bercow.</a></p> <p>But after last night's vote, there will be no public record of an MP being arrested unless the MP outs him or herself.</p> <p>
<figure class="inline-image clearfix ">
<div class="image-credit-wrapper">
<span class="image-credit">BBC</span>

alt="Chair of the Procedure Committee, Charles Walker, gave an impassioned speech backing and praising John Bercow during a debate in parliament about changes to the way the Speaker is elected"
class="captify"
title="Defence: Charles Walker dismissed anger over the new rules as &#034;faux sound and fury&#034;"/>
</div>
<figcaption>Defence: Charles Walker dismissed anger over the new rules as "faux sound and fury"
</figcaption>
</figure>
</p> <p>The motion was tabled by Tory Leader of the House <a href="http://mirror.co.uk/all-about/">Chris Grayling</a>, arguing the naming of MPs under arrest breached their &quot;right to privacy&quot; under the Human Rights Act.</p> <p>While he was Justice Secretary, Mr Grayling led Government plans to scrap the Human Rights Act.</p> <p>Tory MP Charles Walker, who chairs the Commons Procedure Committee, dismissed <a data-action="7325906" data-content-type="news" data-track="false" data-type="inline" data-word-count="5" href="http://www.mirror.co.uk/news/uk-news/watchdog-under-pressure-name-mps-7325906">anger over the rule change</a> as &quot;faux sound and fury&quot;.</p> <p>But Labour's John Mann was a lonely voice of opposition to the change.</p> <p>He told the Mirror: &quot;It gives an additional level of protection to MPs that is unwarranted.</p> <p>&quot;If an MP is arrested on child abuse charges then it should be made public. Indeed, now this could happen and a general election could take place with voters not knowing.</p> <p>&quot;Of course, MPs are innocent until proven guilty, but if the law is to be changed, it should be for everyone, not just MPs.&quot;</p> <p>
<figure class="inline-image clearfix ">
<div class="image-credit-wrapper">
<span class="image-credit">PA</span>

</figcaption>
</figure>
