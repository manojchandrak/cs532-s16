                                    <p>Hundreds of people turned out on Monday to pay their final respects to nine-year-old twins Thomas and Paddy O'Driscoll, who died after being fatally stabbed by their brother Jonathan, 21, last week at their home near Charleville.Their older sibling's body was found a short time later in Buttevant, about 10 miles away from the family's Deerpark home.CHAR(13) + CHAR(10) CHAR(13) + CHAR(10)The alarm was raised on Thursday evening when a member of the family arrived home and discovered the youngsters in the detached, pink and white bungalow just off the main Cork-Limerick road.The children were pronounced dead at the scene. A major police search was then launched for Jonathan, who was found dead in a forested area near a river at Buttevant.Speaking at the funeral mass for the three brothers, Fr Tom Naughton, from Holy Cross Church in Charleville, said: &quot;The twins were loveable rogues. They were fun loving and energetic.&quot;He also said that the day Jonathan came into [adopted parents] Helen and Thomas's lives &quot;was the happiest day of their lives.&quot;The twins' white coffins were placed at either side of their brother's in the chapel for the mass. Floral tributes, a horseshoe, racing car and hurling sticks were placed beside the coffins.Concelebrating the service was Bishop of Cloyne, William Crean and Father Eamon O'Brien, who instructed the twins for their First Holy Communion in May.Fr Naughton said that it's important for people to speak about what is troubling them.&quot;If these days have taught us anything at all, the challenge is to continue and to increase our efforts to understand and to assist, especially young males, to communicate the darkness that seems to be increasing young minds and hearts.&quot;The twins were buried in the Holy Cross graveyard while Jonathan was due to be buried later on Monday in the nearby parish of Kilmallock, where his maternal grandmother's grave lies.
