<p class="entry-meta"> <time class="entry-time" itemprop="datePublished" datetime="2016-02-10T13:42:46+00:00">Posted on February 10, 2016</time> <time class="entry-time" itemprop="datePublished" datetime="2016-02-10T13:42:46+00:00">at 1:42 pm</time> <span class="entry-author" itemprop="author" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">by <a href="http://news.tfw2005.com/author/silveroptimus/" class="entry-author-link" itemprop="url" rel="author"><span class="entry-author-name" itemprop="name">Silver Optimus</span></a></span> <span class="entry-categories">under <a href="http://news.tfw2005.com/video-games/" rel="category tag">Video Games</a></span></p></header><div class="entry-content" itemprop="description articleBody"><span itemscope="itemscope" itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject"><a itemprop="contentUrl" href="http://news.tfw2005.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/10/2016/02/Transformers-Earth-Wars-Windblade.jpg" title="Transformers Earth Wars Windblade" target="_blank" class="fancybox" rel="fancybox-1"><img width="200" height="113" src="http://news.tfw2005.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/10/2016/02/Transformers-Earth-Wars-Windblade-200x113.jpg" class="alignleft post-image" alt="Transformers Earth Wars Windblade" itemprop="thumbnailUrl" /></a> <meta itemprop="description" content="Transformers Earth Wars Windblade" /></span><p style="text-align: justify">Earlier <a href="http://news.tfw2005.com/2016/02/10/transformers-earth-wars-launches-spring-309323">we brought you the news</a> of <strong>Transformers: Earth Wars</strong> with special courtesy from good folks at <strong>Space Ape Games</strong>, <strong>Hasbro</strong> and <strong>Backflip Studios</strong>. You&#8217;ll be curious to know how this partnership came to be. It just so happens that Games Industry; an online magazine dedicated to the business aspect of video games, has come up with a lengthy article highlighting the new licence agreement and the future of the Transformers Video Games. They&#8217;ve also published images of couple of upcoming characters and both of them being Fembots.</p>
<p style="text-align: justify"><em>&#8220;I think the movie game tie-ins that categorised the later generation of console development don&#8217;t really work for mobile,&#8221; says [Simon Hade; co-founder of Space Ape Games]. &#8220;You can&#8217;t have thoe external dependencies driving the game so rigidly. You get some examples of studios that can build a franchise and turn out sequels regularly, but for the most part a good studio wants to ship a game when it&#8217;s ready. So you want to pick an IP and a strategy that work together, where you can take advantage of the fact that your game is going to be recognised and downloaded by a lot of people because it&#8217;s a Transformers game, but you also want to add to the brand, bring something new to it. At the same time you don&#8217;t to have to tie yourself to a movie release or an E3 announcement. I don&#8217;t think that model will be what defines IP games on mobile in the way that it did on console.</em></p>
<p style="text-align: justify">You can learn a lot more from <a href="http://www.gamesindustry.biz/articles/2016-02-10-console-games-can-be-for-christmas-but-free-to-play-is-for-life">Games Industry</a>.</p>
