<p>Today <a href="http://www.xxlmag.com/tags/chance-the-rapper/">Chance the Rapper</a> took to Twitter to ask his followers a simple question: what are the three best songs on <a href="http://www.xxlmag.com/tags/kanye-west/">Kanye West&#8217;s</a> <em>College Dropout </em>album?</p>
<p>He didn&#8217;t give them much time to answer before providing his own answers: &#8220;All Falls Down,&#8221; &#8220;Family Business,&#8221; and &#8220;We Don&#8217;t Care&#8221; are his personal favorites.</p>
<blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-width="550"><p lang="fr" dir="ltr">Best 3 songs on College Dropout?</p>
<p>&mdash; Chance The Rapper (@chancetherapper) <a href="https://twitter.com/chancetherapper/status/697473450441515008">February 10, 2016</a></p></blockquote>
<p><script async src="//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js" charset="utf-8"></script></p>
<blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-width="550"><p lang="en" dir="ltr">Me personally&#8230; All Falls Down, Family Business, We Don&#39;t Care</p>
<p>&mdash; Chance The Rapper (@chancetherapper) <a href="https://twitter.com/chancetherapper/status/697475461312425984">February 10, 2016</a></p></blockquote>
<p><script async src="//platform.twitter.com/widgets.js" charset="utf-8"></script></p>
<p>When <em>Acid Rap </em>came out in 2013, the opening song <a href="https://soundcloud.com/chancetherapper/good-ass-intro-ft-bj-the?in=chancetherapper/sets/chance-the-rapper-acid-rap" target="_blank">&#8220;Good Ass Intro&#8221;</a> was recognized as having a strong Kanye influence. With its partial gospel flavor combined with some juke rhythms, &#8220;Good Ass Intro&#8221; might have even been a prescient sign of the music to come from Yeezy, since he&#8217;s calling his new LP a &#8220;gospel&#8221; album.</p>
<p>Chance and Kanye have been spotted in the studio before and Big Boy teased Chance&#8217;s name when he <a href="http://www.xxlmag.com/news/2016/01/kanye-west-chance-the-rapper-good-fridays/" target="_blank">snapped the first pic of Kanye&#8217;s notepad</a> in the studio. At the top of the year <a href="https://twitter.com/chancetherapper/status/685652227294285824" target="_blank">Chance also joked</a> about artists finding out they aren&#8217;t on the new Yeezy album.</p>
<p>Anyone who&#8217;s seen Chance the Rapper perform live might have been able to guess that &#8220;Family Business&#8221; is one of his favorite cuts from <em>College Dropout</em>; in 2015 he premiered a live cover version of the track at the Summer&#8217;s End Music Festival in Arizona, followed by a <a href="https://youtu.be/ZCI2cK7AeEg" target="_blank">studio version of the song</a> with an accompanying video clip.</p>
<p>Whether Chance the Rapper will be on Kanye West&#8217;s upcoming album remains to be seen, but it&#8217;d be a bit of a disappointment if he&#8217;s not. The trajectory of his past work meshes well with Kanye&#8217;s experimental style, so it&#8217;d make a lot of sense to see these two Chitown natives collaborate.</p>
