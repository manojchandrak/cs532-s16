	On Tuesday, I was on a Black Lives Matter panel at the Facebook headquarters in Palo Alto. It was a private event just for Facebook employees. The campus was amazing and it was a cool experience to be able to see where a platform that I love and use so much is managed on a day to day basis. What I will remember most, though, were several private discussions that I had with both Facebook staff members and a very respected member of our panel.</p>
<p>
	Without fail, each of them expressed to me that they actually really, really like Bernie Sanders. They said that their own personal political views lined up way more with his than they did with Hillary Clinton’s or any of the Republican candidates. They said they loved that Bernie was against the death penalty and for raising the minimum wage to $15 an hour. Bernie, to them, is pretty damn amazing.</p>
<p>
	In spite of all of that, they were pretty sure, though, that they were going to vote for Hillary Clinton.</p>
                           <div class="a-module" itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject" itemid="http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.2527179.1455131752!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_635/dem-2016-sanders.jpg"> <figure class="a-image h">        <img alt='Bernie Sanders beat Hillary Clinton in Tuesday’s New Hampshire primary.' src='http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.2527179.1455131752!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_635/dem-2016-sanders.jpg' />     <span class="a-credit" itemprop="copyrightHolder">John Minchillo/AP</span>     <h2 class="a-caption" itemprop="description">Bernie Sanders beat Hillary Clinton in Tuesday’s New Hampshire primary.</h2>    <a class="a-enlarge" href="#"></a>   </figure> </div>        
<p>
	<a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/news/politics/bernie-sanders-beats-hillary-clinton-new-hampshire-primar-article-1.2526095" target="_blank"><strong>BERNIE SANDERS CRUSHES HILLARY CLINTON IN N.H. PRIMARY</strong></a></p>
<p>
	When I asked why in the world they would go against their own views and preferences, I always got some version of the same answer:</p>
<p>
	“Bernie Sanders could not win in a general election.”</p>
<p>
	Not a single one of them had any hard demographic data or poll results or inside information that caused them to believe this. Instead, their views were all hinged on conventional wisdom.</p>
<p>
	I get it.</p>
<p>
	Eight years ago, 16 years ago, I don’t think Bernie Sanders could’ve won the Democratic nomination, much less the Presidential election, but 2016 is different.</p>
                           <div class="a-module" itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject" itemid="http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.2527174.1455131746!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_635/facebook-privacy-myth-reality.jpg"> <figure class="a-image h">     <img alt='A JUNE 11, 2014 FILE PHOTO' src='http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.2527174.1455131746!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_635/facebook-privacy-myth-reality.jpg' />     <span class="a-credit" itemprop="copyrightHolder">Jeff Chiu/AP</span>     <h2 class="a-caption" itemprop="description">At the Facebook headquarters in Palo Alto many staff members discussed how much they liked Bernie Sanders.</h2>    <a class="a-enlarge" href="#"></a>   </figure> </div>        
<p>
	Conventional wisdom has its place, but in this political climate, it’s a poor substitute for an accurate prediction. Let me make the case for how Bernie Sanders becomes the next American president.</p>
<p>
	<strong>Young People &amp; Women</strong></p>
<p>
	Yesterday, in New Hampshire, an <a href="http://thinkprogress.org/politics/2016/02/09/3747988/bernie-sanders-primary-winner/" target="_blank">astounding 84% of young people</a> ages 17 to 29 voted for Bernie Sanders. 82% of young women under the age of 30 voted for Bernie yesterday in New Hampshire.</p>
<p>
	The <a href="http://fivethirtyeight.com/features/why-young-democrats-love-bernie-sanders/" target="_blank">same numbers were true in Iowa</a>. Young voters aren’t just marginally breaking for Bernie — it’s not even close.</p>
<p>
	It’s not just super young voters who love and respect Bernie Sanders, either. In Iowa, voters ages 30 to 44 chose Bernie over Hillary <a href="http://fivethirtyeight.com/features/why-young-democrats-love-bernie-sanders/" target="_blank">by a 21% margin</a>. In New Hampshire, this demographic <a href="http://graphics.wsj.com/elections/2016/new-hampshire-primaries-exit-polls/" target="_blank">was even more pronounced</a> — Bernie won voters ages 30 to 44 by a 66% to 32% margin. On Tuesday, in a widely shared op-ed for The Guardian, RoseAnn DeMoro <a href="http://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2016/feb/09/i-am-a-woman-voting-for-best-feminist-bernie-sanders-president-hillary-clinton" target="_blank">said</a>, “I’m a woman and I will vote for the best feminist for president: Bernie Sanders. I won’t vote against Hillary Clinton because she’s female, but I don’t intend to vote for her because I am. We need more fundamental changes in this country.”</p>
                           <div class="a-module" itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject" itemid="http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.2527180.1455131753!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_635/509278764.jpg"> <figure class="a-image h">        <img alt='Many voters say they will vote for Hillary Clinton as the Democratic presidential nominee because Bernie Sanders could not win in a general election.' src='http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.2527180.1455131753!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_635/509278764.jpg' />     <span class="a-credit" itemprop="copyrightHolder">Darren McCollester/Getty Images</span>     <h2 class="a-caption" itemprop="description">Many voters say they will vote for Hillary Clinton as the Democratic presidential nominee because Bernie Sanders could not win in a general election.</h2>    <a class="a-enlarge" href="#"></a>   </figure> </div>        
<p>
	<strong>The Trust Factor</strong></p>
<p>
	We live in an age where people just don’t trust conventional politicians. Bernie Sanders, though, may be the most trusted politician in America.</p>
<p>
	Asked which politician was “honest and trustworthy,” New Hampshire voters chose Bernie Sanders over Hillary Clinton 91% to 5%. Remember, Hillary only lost the state by 21%, so what we’re seeing is that even huge volumes of her own voters <a href="http://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2016/feb/09/i-am-a-woman-voting-for-best-feminist-bernie-sanders-president-hillary-clinton" target="_blank">admit they actually trust Bernie more</a>. That is almost identical to what I heard from my conversations at Facebook.</p>
<p>
	In fact, polls have determined that Bernie Sanders is the only candidate from either party with <a href="http://fivethirtyeight.com/features/donald-trump-is-really-unpopular-with-general-election-voters/" target="_blank">a net positive favorability rating</a>.</p>
<p>
	Guess who the three least liked candidates are nationally? In this order — Donald Trump, Jeb Bush and Hillary Clinton.</p>
<p>
	<strong style="-webkit-padding-start: 0px; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; outline: 0px; font-size: 14px; vertical-align: baseline; box-sizing: border-box; color: rgb(17, 17, 17); font-family: Arial, sans-serif; line-height: 21px; background-image: initial; background-attachment: initial; background-size: initial; background-origin: initial; background-clip: initial; background-position: initial; background-repeat: initial;">FULL COVERAGE:<a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/news/election-2016" style="-webkit-padding-start: 0px; margin: 0px; padding: 0px; vertical-align: baseline; transition: color 300ms ease-in-out; text-decoration: none; color: rgb(1, 95, 182); box-sizing: border-box; background: transparent;" target="_blank">THE 2016 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTION</a></strong></p>
<p>
	Just as Democrats of every persuasion will rally against someone like Donald Trump, Republicans of every persuasion will do so against Hillary Clinton. This notion that come a general election Clinton is going to become someone that the majority of Americans trust or have a favorable view on just isn’t based in fact.</p>
<p>
	<strong>Independents</strong></p>
<p>
	An increasing number of Americans don’t closely identify themselves with any political party. Bernie Sanders is the longest serving Independent congressman in the history of America. When he speaks of being free from corporate interests, he has been able to say this for so long, in great part, by also staying free from the burdensome restraints of the Democratic party. This is what gave him the freedom to vote against the wars in Iraq and Afghanistan. This is what gave him the freedom to vote against the draconian welfare bill famously touted by Bill Clinton. This is how he has been able to continue to say he’s against the death penalty. Bernie Sanders has been independent his entire political career and has received virtually zero support from the party elites this primary season.</p>
<p>
	Consequently, independents are coming out for Bernie in big numbers — which is essential in any Presidential election. In New Hampshire, <a href="https://twitter.com/meetthepress/status/697230331246743554?ref_src=twsrc^tfw" target="_blank">72% of Independents broke for Bernie</a>. In Iowa he <a href="http://www.dailykos.com/story/2016/2/3/1479527/-Bernie-killing-it-with-Independents-in-Iowa-and-New-Hampshire-so-far" target="_blank">won 69% of Independents</a>.</p>
<p>
	Head to Head, Bernie Does Better</p>
<p>
	In every available poll showing Bernie Sanders running against every Republican candidate and Hillary Clinton running against those candidates, Bernie outperforms Hillary in all of them. This was true in <a href="http://www.dailykos.com/story/2015/10/27/1440343/-The-Electability-Argument-Is-Dead-All-the-Polls-Show-Bernie-Does-Better-Against-GOP-Than-Hillary">October</a>. This was true in <a href="http://thehill.com/blogs/ballot-box/presidential-races/261756-poll-sanders-more-electable-than-hillary-against-top" target="_blank">December</a>. It was true in <a href="http://www.cnn.com/2016/01/20/politics/bernie-sanders-hillary-clinton-gop-nh/" target="_blank">January</a>. Some key Republican leaders are actually starting to say they think <a href="http://time.com/4182980/republicans-rnc-hillary-clinton-bernie-sanders/" target="_blank">they’d prefer to go against Hillary</a>.</p>
                           <div class="a-module" itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject" itemid="http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.2527181.1455131754!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_635/dem-2016-sanders.jpg"> <figure class="a-image h">        <img alt='Attendees cheer after  Sen. Bernie Sanders spoke during a campaign stop at the Pinkerton Academy Stockbridge Theatre in Derry, N.H. on Monday.' src='http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.2527181.1455131754!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_635/dem-2016-sanders.jpg' />     <span class="a-credit" itemprop="copyrightHolder">John Minchillo/AP</span>     <h2 class="a-caption" itemprop="description">Attendees cheer after  Sen. Bernie Sanders spoke during a campaign stop at the Pinkerton Academy Stockbridge Theatre in Derry, N.H. on Monday.</h2>    <a class="a-enlarge" href="#"></a>   </figure> </div>        
<p>
	While the Bernie Sanders campaign has worked hard to promote these head to head polls, the information just hasn’t trickled down enough for people on the ground to know that this is the case.</p>
<p>
	Prominent African-American voters are turning toward Bernie</p>
<p>
	Bernie Sanders, particularly compared to Hillary Clinton, has not been well known for very long among most African-Americans. As people have time to investigate and understand the differences between the two, the tide will start to really turn.</p>
<p>
	Just today, Ta-Nehisi Coates, perhaps the most respected and widely read author of issues important to black voters, <a href="http://www.democracynow.org/2016/2/10/ta_nehisi_coates_is_voting_for?autostart=true" target="_blank">said he intends to vote for Bernie Sanders</a>.</p>
<p>
	Michelle Alexander, the legal scholar who authored “The New Jim Crow,” which may be the most quoted and leaned on book of the Black Lives Matter Movement, <a href="http://www.thenation.com/article/hillary-clinton-does-not-deserve-black-peoples-votes/" target="_blank">wrote a scathing op-ed</a> entitled, “Hillary Clinton Does Not Deserve the Black Vote.”</p>
                           <div class="a-module" itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject" itemid="http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.2527178.1455131751!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_635/nup-169854-0078.jpg"> <figure class="a-image h">     <img alt='FOR EDITORIAL USE ONLY. <p>
	Ben Jealous, the former head of the NAACP, and a widely respected and connected organizer, endorsed Bernie Sanders last week and <a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/news/politics/naacp-chief-ben-jealous-endorse-bernie-sanders-article-1.2520128" target="_blank">has been working full-force on the campaign trail</a>.</p>
<p>
	And the Rev. Al Sharpton met with Bernie at Sylvia’s, a Harlem soul food mecca, on Wednesday morning, just hours after Bernie’s commanding win in New Hampshire.</p>
<p>
	<a href="http://www.nydailynews.com/news/politics/bernie-sanders-meets-rev-al-sharpton-harlem-article-1.2526678" target="_blank"><strong>BERNIE SANDERS MEETS WITH REV. AL SHARPTON IN HARLEM</strong></a></p>
                           <div class="a-module" itemprop="associatedMedia" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/ImageObject" itemid="http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.2527176.1455131750!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_635/kingsanders11n-5-web.jpg"> <figure class="a-image h">        <img alt='Michelle Alexander, the legal scholar who authored “The New Jim Crow,” wrote a scathing op-ed entitled, “Hillary Clinton Does Not Deserve the Black Vote.”' src='http://assets.nydailynews.com/polopoly_fs/1.2527176.1455131750!/img/httpImage/image.jpg_gen/derivatives/article_635/kingsanders11n-5-web.jpg' />     <span class="a-credit" itemprop="copyrightHolder">Michelle Alexander/facebook</span>     <h2 class="a-caption" itemprop="description">Michelle Alexander, the legal scholar who authored “The New Jim Crow,” wrote a scathing op-ed entitled, “Hillary Clinton Does Not Deserve the Black Vote.”</h2>    <a class="a-enlarge" href="#"></a>   </figure> </div>        
<p>
	While no single individual represents or speaks for all of black America, these four men and women are among the most respected, trusted, experienced leaders and thinkers in the country. Cornel West, another leading scholar, has already been on the trail with Bernie for months.</p>
<p>
	When the leading scholars, thinkers, and organizers are starting to break for Bernie, it will have a ripple effect.</p>
<p>
	In my opinion, the candidate who people trust, who strongly wins the youth and independent vote, who beats the opponents better head to head, and who earnestly garners the support of African-Americans, is a winning candidate.</p>
<p>
	It seems crazy that a 74-year-old man who has called himself a Democratic Socialist for most of his life could become our next president, but the path is clear, and it is very possible.</p>
