<p align="justify"><strong>U.S.</strong> Republican presidential contender <strong>Donald Trump</strong> said <strong>German</strong> Chancellor <strong>Angela Merkel</strong> was wrong to let in thousands of migrants into <strong>Germany</strong> and that the refugee crisis could trigger revolutions and even the end of <strong>Europe</strong>.</p>
<p align="justify">&quot;<em>I think Angela Merkel made a tragic mistake with the migrants</em>,&quot; Trump told French conservative weekly <strong>Valeurs Actuelles</strong>, which said it was the billionaire&#8217;s first in-depth campaign interview with European media.</p>
<p align="justify">&quot;<em>If you don&#8217;t treat the situation competently and firmly, yes, it&#8217;s the end of Europe. You could face real revolutions,</em>&quot; Trump was quoted as saying, according to the <strong>French</strong> translation.</p><div class="insert-post-ads" style="clear:both;float:left;width:100%;margin:0 0 20px 0;"><centre><script async src="//pagead2.googlesyndication.com/pagead/js/adsbygoogle.js"></script>

<p align="justify">The 69-year-old property magnate also said <strong>Brussels</strong> had become a breeding ground for terrorists and some neighbourhoods in Paris and elsewhere in France had become no-go zones.</p>
<p align="justify">&quot;<em>Unfortunately, France is not what it used to be, and neither is Paris,</em>&quot; he said. He also said tight French gun laws were partly responsible for the killing of dozens of people at the Bataclan concert hall last November by Islamist militants.</p>
<p align="justify">&quot;<em>I always have a gun with me. Had I been at the Bataclan, I can tell you I would have opened fire</em>,&quot; he said.</p>
<p align="justify">Trump further said he thought the United States could have very good relations with Russia&#8217;s Vladimir Putin and that nothing could be worse than the current situation where President <strong>Barack Obama</strong> and Putin scarcely spoke with each other.</p>
<p align="justify">&quot;<em>He (Putin) said I was brilliant. That proves a certain smartness</em>,&quot; said Trump.</p>
<p align="justify">The French magazine said the interview was conducted at Trump&#8217;s office in New York&#8217;s Trump Tower a week before the Iowa caucuses, in which he finished second among candidates seeking the Republican nomination for November&#8217;s presidential election.</p>
<p align="justify">Trump was widely expected to win Tuesday&#8217;s primary in New Hampshire, which is part of the state-by-state process of picking party nominees for the Nov. 8 election to replace Democratic President Barack Obama. </p>
