            <h1>By Scott Van Voorhis</h1>
            <p>Boston.com Correspondent</p>

    <div class="date">February 9, 2016 3:13 PM</div>
    
    <p class="generic-p-tag">A relatively mild start to winter dramatically boosted home sales activity as buyers took advantage of the spring-like temps to hit open houses and make offers.</p>
<p class="generic-p-tag">The number of pending sales&mdash;where homes have been put under agreement, but have not yet officially closed &ndash; rose nearly 40 percent in January compared to the same month a year ago, the Massachusetts Association of Realtors (MAR) reports.</p>
<p class="generic-p-tag">It was a sharp contrast with last winter&rsquo;s epic arctic weather and never ending blizzards, which sent pending sales last January plummeting 16 percent while effectively burying the spring sales market under several feet of snow.</p>
<p class="generic-p-tag">With the exception of last January, pending sales have been up every month for nearly three years now, the realtors group notes.</p>
<p class="generic-p-tag">Even as sales shot up this January, prices remained relatively flat, edging up .1 percent to $330,000.</p>
<p class="generic-p-tag">Pending condo sales were also up as well, rising 34 percent, with the median condo price edging down .8 percent to $309,438, MAR reports.</p>
<p class="generic-p-tag">
<strong>Increasing confidence</strong>

</p>
<p class="generic-p-tag">&ldquo;Buyers were again out in force in January and were able to take advantage of the mild weather to see homes and make a high number of offers that were accepted in January,&rdquo; said 2016 MAR President Annie Blatz, branch executive at Kinlin Grover Real Estate on Cape Cod, in a press statement.</p>
<p class="generic-p-tag">&ldquo;A still improving economy and an increase in first-time homebuyers in Massachusetts added to the buyer demand,&rdquo; she said.</p>
<p class="generic-p-tag">Real estate brokers are experiencing growing confidence in the overall market, though less so with the direction of prices.</p>
<p class="generic-p-tag">MAR&rsquo;s Market and Price Confidence Indexes show a 6 percent increase in market confidence, to 66.9, but pricing confidence remaining essentially flat at 73.58. The indexes are on a 100 point scale, with 50 the midpoint between a weak market (0) and a strong market, 100.</p>
<p class="generic-p-tag">
<strong>Smart homes making ripples</strong>

</p>
<p class="generic-p-tag">Meanwhile, real estate brokers are also increasingly selling homes with &ldquo;smart home&rdquo; features, according to a poll by MAR of its members.</p>
<p class="generic-p-tag">A little below 40 percent of Realtors polled said they had listed a home with smart home features that included &ldquo;thermostats, security systems and lighting that are controllable by an internet-connected device,&rdquo; according to the survey.</p>
<p class="generic-p-tag">A very small number, 2 percent, said most of the homes they listed had smart home features, while the majority, 58 percent, said the haven&rsquo;t seen any smart home gadgets in the homes they are selling.</p>
<p class="generic-p-tag">&ldquo;These smart home devices are still relatively new to the marketplace, but are starting to increase in popularity here in Massachusetts,&rdquo; Blatz noted. &ldquo;It&rsquo;s a good bet that we will see their use rise as more people see the benefits.&rdquo; </p></div></div>
