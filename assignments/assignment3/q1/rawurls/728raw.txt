<p><a href="http://www.dailykos.com/story/2016/02/10/1470292/-In-Defense-of-Farmers-Ranchers" target="_blank" title="">In Defense of Farmers/Ranchers</a> written by <strong>Nodin</strong>: “My first diary contains an image of the Shideler Ranch where I spent a lot of hours as a kid bucking bales of hay, herding cattle or just plain doing gruntwork on fencing or stockyard cleaning. I want to give you a fairer perception of the small farmer/rancher across America, one that is <em>not</em> in any way exemplified by the Bundys of recent notoriety. This diary contains my own experiences for the most part, along with paraphrasing those of my extended farming/ranching family. The rancher in the picture is my old family friend, Barry Shideler (recently deceased) and his wife Marilyn. [...] Small farmers and ranchers are a severely endangered species with the rise of phenomenally huge Agra. My friend Barry pastured his own cattle and the ranch did lease BLM land for grazing. Barry’s great grandpa had a 99 year lease on the land so I would imagine that lease expired about the time Barry inherited the entire responsibility. I don’t know if it was renewed but my guess is it had to be or the ranch would not have survived. The lease cost used to be manageable, but I suspect it has grown right along with taxes, both of which cut into profits a rancher might make which are all too often not enough for expenses that continually rise.”</p>

<p><a href="http://www.dailykos.com/story/2016/02/10/1482872/-Meanwhile-the-Supreme-Court-deals-a-severe-blow-to-humanity-s-future-Google-it" target="_blank" title="">Meanwhile, the Supreme Court deals a severe blow to humanity's future. Google it</a><span> written by </span><strong>citisven</strong><span>: </span><span>“</span><span>Those are the exact words of a text message I got from one of my friends at the EPA last night, in response to this: </span><em>In a major setback for President Obama’s climate change agenda, the Supreme Court on Tuesday temporarily blocked the administration’s effort to combat global warming by regulating emissions from coal-fired power plants.</em><span> </span><span>It puts yesterday’s </span><em>New York Times</em><span> </span><a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2016/02/10/us/politics/supreme-court-blocks-obama-epa-coal-emissions-regulations.html" target="_blank" title="">headline</a><span> into a larger context of what the Supreme Court’s decision to issue a stay on the Obama Administration’s efforts to regulate coal powered plants could really mean. If this holds, it’s not only going to derail the EPA’s</span><a href="http://www.epa.gov/cleanpowerplan/regulatory-actions#regulations" target="_blank" title="">comprehensive efforts</a><span> to clean up the worst CO2 emitters in this country, but the effects of this reckless decision will ripple throughout the world, right into the painstaking details of an already fickle </span><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paris_Agreement" target="_blank" title="">climate agreement</a><span> negotiated in Paris. If the US has to renege on its commitment to get serious about curbing its own emissions, it is quite possibly going to undo the thousands upon thousands of pieces put into place across the global board like a flick of the first domino.”</span></p>

<p class="AlignCenter"><strong>CRITTERS AND THE GREAT OUTDOORS</strong></p>

<p><a href="http://www.dailykos.com/story/2016/02/04/1479815/-The-first-video-of-an-American-jaguar-is-released" target="_blank" title="">The first video of an American jaguar is released</a> written by <strong>Walter Einenkel:</strong> “The <a href="http://www.biologicaldiversity.org/news/press_releases/2016/jaguar-02-03-2016.html" target="_blank" title="">Center for Biological Diversity</a> released video showing the first ever footage of an American jaguar. Turns out, he lives in Arizona! <em>The camera project is part of ongoing efforts to monitor mountain ranges in southeastern Arizona for endangered jaguar and ocelot. Chris Bugbee, a biologist with Conservation CATalyst, has been collecting data on the Santa Rita jaguar for the past three years (formerly through the University of Arizona). ‘Studying these elusive cats anywhere is extremely difficult, but following the only known individual in the U.S. is especially challenging,’ said Bugbee. ‘We use our specially trained scat detection dog and spent three years tracking in rugged mountains, collecting data and refining camera sites; these videos represent the peak of our efforts.’</em>The jaguar has been named El Jefe (‘the boss,’ en español) and lives in the Santa Rita mountains, about 25 miles away from downtown Tucson.”</p>

<p><a href="http://www.dailykos.com/story/2016/02/04/1480102/-Antarctica-s-Scar-Inlet-Ice-Shelf-is-anticipated-to-shatter-by-the-end-of-March" target="_blank" title="">Antarctica's Scar Inlet Ice Shelf is anticipated to shatter by the end of March</a> written by <strong>Pakalolo</strong>: “<span>The Scar Inlet Ice Shelf will likely fall apart by the end of Antarctica’s summer, predicted Ted Scambos, a glaciologist at the </span><a href="https://nsidc.org/" target="_blank" title="">National Snow and Ice Data Center</a><span> in Boulder, Colorado. This inlet's ice is the largest remnant of the vast Larsen B shelf that is still attached to the Antarctica peninsula. One small fragment, the Seal Nunataks, clings on as well. In the Southern Hemisphere's summer of 2002, about 1,250 square miles of the enormous Larsen B Ice Shelf splintered into hundreds of icebergs. Scar Inlet is about two-thirds the size of the ice lost from Larsen B and it to is expected to shatter into thousands of pieces. This ice sheet formed over thousands of years as ice sheets and glaciers flow off the land and extend into the ocean to create a thick floating platform of ice. Scar Inlet ice shelf acts as a dam, holding back the glaciers. When this ice shelf disintegrates, it will unleash the glaciers ice and shelf ice at increasing rates into the ocean, which contributes to rising sea level.</span>”</p>

<p><a href="http://www.dailykos.com/story/2016/02/03/1478376/-Goodbye-Gondwana-Remnants-of-ancient-ecosystem-torch-in-Tasmania-due-to-climate-change" target="_blank" title="">Goodbye Gondwana: Remnants of ancient ecosystem torch in Tasmania due to climate change</a> written by <strong>Besame</strong>: “This January, Earth lost plants and peat soils that are remaining fragments of a ecosystem from the Cretaceous period. Found only in Tasmania, these particular plants and soils took a thousand years or more to grow and form, and in one month many disappeared in wildfires, perhaps forever. Scientists point at climate change as the underlying cause of recent wildfires in the UNESCO <u><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tasmanian_Wilderness_World_Heritage_Area" title="">Tasmania Wilderness World Heritage Area (WHA)</a></u> that preserves the last ecosystem remnants from the<a href="http://www.livescience.com/37285-gondwana.html" title=""><u>supercontinent Gondwana</u></a> that broke apart 180 million years ago. The fires began in mid-January when extensive dry lightning struck Tasmania’s high-altitude rainforests and bogs that were uncharacteristically dry due to record-breaking low rainfall. These ancient habitats survive on western Tasmania’s high central plateau protected by its wet climate and the absence of the fire-dependent vegetation found elsewhere in Australia. Fire hadn’t been a regular part of this environment for many millions of years.”</p>

I love the seasons where I live, don’t get me wrong. I don’t think I’d appreciate spring as much. It’s not so hard in December, when the year is at its darkest in the pre-holiday frenzy. I find solace in the cheerful twinkling of the lights on every house and building. In January, the darkness persists but the lights are extinguished. I am the first one up in the morning, and this winter I have had to shovel a path down the driveway for the paper more often than not. On clear mornings the stars in the constellation Orion hang in the pre-dawn sky like so many sharp little points of ice. However, if you asked me, I’d vote for warm weather and green grass every time. The world eventually turns towards that goal, and I start to revive and cheer up when it becomes apparent that the weather is finally going to start warming up and I can look forward to spring. I think I’ve made it through the darkness into the light once again. Indoor flowers also sustain us gardeners while we watch the sun turn back towards the northern sky. I rescued an amaryllis from the local Walmart in January of 2015, and it bloomed for me this year.”</p>

<p class="AlignCenter"><strong>TRANSPORTATION &amp; INFRASTRUCTURE</strong></p>

<p><a href="http://www.dailykos.com/story/2016/02/08/1481359/-Funding-a-clean-transportation-system" target="_blank" title="">Funding a clean transportation system</a> written by<strong> ybruti</strong>: “<span>T</span><span>o fund </span><a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/the-press-office/2016/02/04/fact-sheet-president-obamas-21st-century-clean-transportation-system" target="_blank" title="">a 21st Century Clean Transportation System</a><span>,</span><span> </span><span>the Obama administration is proposing a $10 per barrel fee on oil, gradually phased in over five years, which would bring in </span><a href="http://www.nytimes.com/2016/02/05/business/energy-environment/obama-to-propose-a-10-a-barrel-fee-on-oil.html?_r=0" target="_blank" title="">$32 billion annually</a><span>. This would support clean vehicle research, ensure the Highway Trust Fund remains solvent, and</span><span> improve public transit</span><span>. </span><span>It would also encourage innovation, strengthen the economy, create hundreds of thousands of new jobs, and assist families with energy costs. </span><span>Most importantly, since 30% of U.S. greenhouse gas emissions are related to transportation, a cleaner system would help fight global warming. </span>[...] However, Steve Scales of Louisiana, the Majority Whip in the House, says the oil fee is <a href="http://thehill.com/policy/energy-environment/268266-obama-to-push-10-per-barrel-oil-tax-for-green-transportation-plan" target="_blank" title="">an absurd proposal</a> that will be dead on arrival. But it should not be dismissed so quickly. The results of a carbon fee (or tax) in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Columbia_carbon_tax" target="_blank" title="">British Columbia</a> since 2008 show that gasoline use has declined there and the economy has benefited.”</p>

<p><a href="http://www.dailykos.com/story/2016/02/04/1480046/-Obama-proposes-oil-tax-to-pay-for-public-transportation" target="_blank" title="">Obama proposes oil tax to pay for public transportation</a> written by <strong>gjohnsit</strong>: “Some of us on DKos were talking about this just the other day. That the government should tax some of the price collapse in energy to make the country more energy efficient. It seems the president was <a href="http://www.bloomberg.com/politics/articles/2016-02-04/obama-to-request-10-per-barrel-oil-tax-for-transportation-needs" target="_blank" title="">thinking the same thing.</a> President Barack Obama will propose a $10 per barrel tax on oil in his fiscal 2017 budget to finance self-driving cars, public transit, railroads and other transportation improvements, the White House said. ‘By placing a fee on oil, the President’s plan creates a clear incentive for private sector innovation to reduce our reliance on oil and at the same time invests in clean energy technologies that will power our future,’ the White House said Thursday in a statement. [...] <span>Obviously the Republic-controlled Congress is unlikely to agree with this common sense idea, but if no one ever even suggests it then a “slim chance” becomes “no chance” (like universal health care).</span>”</p>

<p><a href="http://www.dailykos.com/story/2016/02/05/1480168/-Finally-Obama-proposes-a-10-per-barrel-fee-on-oil" target="_blank" title="">Finally! Obama proposes a $10 per barrel fee on oil</a> written by <strong>Magnifico</strong>: “In his budget delivered to Congress, President Obama proposed a new few oil companies that would assess $10 for every barrel of oil both domestically extracted as well as imports. The fee would be phased in over five years and the money raised by it could then be used to fund repairs on America’s aging transportation infrastructure, and clean transportation research and development. Such as building high-speed rail, more environmentally friendly airplanes, electric-car charging stations, and improving electric vehicles. The president’s goal is a “clean transportation” system. The White House released a fact sheet describing the president’s plan for a <strong><a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/the-press-office/2016/02/04/fact-sheet-president-obamas-21st-century-clean-transportation-system" target="_blank" title="">21st Century Clean Transportation System</a></strong>, characterizing it as ‘smart, strategic integrated investments to help reduce carbon pollution, strengthen [the] economy.’”</p>

<p><a href="http://www.dailykos.com/story/2016/02/04/1480044/-NC-Dept-Of-Transportation-Nixes-Red-Route-Environmentalists-Protest-Republican-Spin-On-This" target="_blank" title="">NC Dept. Of Transportation Nixes Red Route, Environmentalists Protest, Republican Spin On This...</a>written by <strong>Kalisiin</strong>: “<em>This comes straight from an email I received today from Chad Barefoot, my State Senator. The North Carolina Department of Transportation announced Wednesday that it's recommending routes for the final segment of the Complete 540 Project, and their recommendations do not include the controversial "Red Route" that would have split communities throughout Garner and Southern Wake County. Instead, the department recommended using the orange, green and mint routes. Environmentalists acted immediately to criticize&lt;<a href="http://www.newsobserver.com/news/local/counties/wake-county/article58125663.html" target="_blank" title="">www.newsobserver.com/...</a>&gt; the Department of Transportation's decision to prioritize people over plants</em>.”</p>

<p><a href="http://www.dailykos.com/story/2016/02/08/1481468/-O-Malley-Hillary-Would-Literally-Destroy-the-Planet" target="_blank" title="">O'Malley: Hillary's Climate Plan Would Literally Destroy the Planet</a> written by <strong>New Minas</strong>: “Martin O’Malley <a href="https://newrepublic.com/article/128179/martin-omalley-says-hillary-clinton-will-let-planet-literally-burn-up" target="_blank" title="">minces no words</a>:  <em>Her goal would be to reach 33 percent of clean energy in the electric grid by 2027, up from 16 percent today. ‘Secretary Clinton’s plan can most kindly be summed up as a voluntary solar panel plan for residences,” O’Malley told me. “Under her plan the planet would literally burn up.<strong> </strong>Her approach is much more incremental.’</em>  Get that?  A voluntary home solar plan, using free market principles to allow the invisible hand of the market to save the planet. This is the hallmark of corporatist incremental suicide.  We cannot possibly do what needs to be done because it doesn’t fit within the arena of possibilities that are allowed under the consensus of corporate interests.”</p>

<p><a href="http://www.dailykos.com/story/2016/02/04/1480039/-Army-of-Lobbyists-Push-LNG-Exports-Methane-Hydrates-Coal-in-Senate-Energy-Bill" target="_blank" title="">Army of Lobbyists Push LNG Exports, Methane Hydrates, Coal in Senate Energy Bill</a> written by <strong>Steve Horn</strong>: “As the U.S. presidential race dominates the media, it is easy to forget that both chambers of the U.S. Congress are currently in session. The U.S. Senate has put a major energy bill on the table, the <a href="http://oilprice.com/Energy/Energy-General/Congress-Looks-To-Pass-Energy-Bill-for-First-Time-Since-2007.html" target="_blank" title="">first of its sort since 2007</a>. The 237-page bill introduced by U.S. Sen. Lisa Murkowski (R-AK) — <a href="https://www.gpo.gov/fdsys/pkg/BILLS-114s2012pcs/pdf/BILLS-114s2012pcs.pdf" target="_blank" title="">S. 2012, the Energy Policy Modernization Act of 2015</a> — includes provisions that would expedite the liquefied natural gas (LNG) export permitting process, heap subsidies on coal technology, and fund research geared toward discovering a way to tap into <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methane_clathrate" target="_blank" title="">methane hydrate reserves</a>. <a href="http://www.desmogblog.com/2015/12/16/corporate-lobbyists-big-money-oil-exports-ban" target="_blank" title="">As we saw with the lifting of the U.S. crude oil export ban</a>, which was part of a broader congressional budget bill, a DeSmog investigation reveals that these provisions once existed as stand-alone bills pushed for by an army of fossil fuel industry lobbyists. The <a href="https://www.opensecrets.org/lobby/billsum.php?id=s2012-114" target="_blank" title="">list of lobbyists for S. 2012</a> is a who's who of major fossil fuel corporations and their trade associations: BP, ExxonMobil, America's Natural Gas Alliance, American Petroleum Institute, Peabody Energy, Arch Coal, Southern Company, Duke Energy and many other <a href="http://soprweb.senate.gov/index.cfm?event=getFilingDetails&amp;filingID=9C78F065-56B4-45F1-9336-33E71CCB5EC7&amp;filingTypeID=78" target="_blank" title="">prominent</a> <a href="http://soprweb.senate.gov/index.cfm?event=getFilingDetails&amp;filingID=5007181B-6688-48C2-86C7-EE86FC8470A3&amp;filingTypeID=78" target="_blank" title="">LNG</a> <a href="http://soprweb.senate.gov/index.cfm?event=getFilingDetails&amp;filingID=BBB976AB-1002-41CF-8407-C33CDAED8756&amp;filingTypeID=69" target="_blank" title="">export</a> <a href="http://soprweb.senate.gov/index.cfm?event=getFilingDetails&amp;filingID=67FCAFC5-7828-4461-9546-772DEA7C44AE&amp;filingTypeID=78" target="_blank" title="">companies</a>.”</p>

<p><a href="http://www.dailykos.com/stories/2016/2/8/1481798/-NH-Republicans-a-good-match-on-climate-change-wackiness-with-Cruz-Trump-and-Rubio" target="_blank" title="">NH Republicans a good match on climate change wackiness with Cruz, Trump, and Rubio</a> written by <strong>Meteor Blades</strong>: “In a less insane world, Donald Trump, Ted Cruz, and Marco Rubio’s views on climate change would be enough to scrub them right out of the race for the Republican presidential nomination. Cruz's answer—an eight-minute rant that you can watch [<a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/video/politics/cruz-climate-change-issue-is-about-government-power/2016/02/03/2db6adb0-ca93-11e5-b9ab-26591104bb19_video.html" target="_blank" title="">here</a>]—was essentially that he would do nothing. Trump <a href="http://www.eenews.net/stories/1060030238" target="_blank" title="">has repeatedly called</a> global warming a ‘hoax,’ and <a href="http://abcnews.go.com/blogs/politics/2014/05/sen-marco-rubio-yes-im-ready-to-be-president/" target="_blank" title="">Rubio has said</a>, ‘I do not believe that human activity is causing these dramatic changes to our climate the way these scientists are portraying it.’</em>”</p>

<p><a href="http://www.dailykos.com/story/2016/02/04/1479825/-Climate-change-the-optics-and-priorities-of-our-candidates" target="_blank" title="">Carbon footprints - Hillary vs Bernie</a> written by<strong> pianogramma</strong>: “Unlike the Republican pack of climate change deniers, both Hillary and Bernie at least acknowledge the problem. But just as it seems crazy to expect a recipient of millions of dollars from Wall Street to do everything possible to rein in Wall Street, it seems crazy to expect someone who has eagerly acquired a carbon footprint probably a hundred times as big as the average American’s (America’s being twenty times too big already) to do everything possible to rein in the overconsumption underlying our excessive carbon emissions. Pope Francis leads by example on climate change and economic justice. Jimmy Carter does the same. So can Bernie, I believe. You can probably guess which of our candidates I imagine would have been a slaveholder during the 1850’s.”</p>

<p><a href="http://www.dailykos.com/story/2016/02/05/1480158/-Senate-Ds-Sought-a-United-Front-to-Get-More-for-Flint-in-Energy-Bill-These-Five-Broke-Ranks" target="_blank" title="">Senate Ds Sought a United Front to Get More $ for Flint in Energy Bill. Already endorsed by Senator Snowball, aka Jim Inhofe, the man who brought a snowball into Senate chambers to demonstrate climate change is a lie, Rubio, if elected President, will be turning climate denial into climate policy.”</p>

$(function(){
  // almost all of this, except maybe an init line, gets moved to
  // stories.js when we merge in epics/story_authoring_tool,
  // which is why they are hanging out and not namespaced
  var $comments =  $("#comments");

  _.defer(function(){ CommentApp.initHiddenCommentsClickListener($comments); });

  DailyKos.Story.initStoryPageAndLoadComments({
    el:               $comments,
    tree:             $(".comment-tree"),
    storyID:          1481185,
    authorID:         6,
    commentsCloseAt:  new Date(1455924689000),
    commentsPath:     '/stories/1481185/comments'
  });

  var imagesToAlign = findImagesToAlign();

  if(imagesToAlign.length > 0) {
    alignStoryImages(imagesToAlign);

    $(window).resize(function() {
      alignStoryImages(imagesToAlign);
    });
  }

  function findImagesToAlign() {
    return _.filter($('.dkimg-r'), function(image) {
      return $(image).parents('blockquote, table').length === 0;
    });
  };

  function alignStoryImages(imagesToAlign) {
    var contentOffset = $('.content-section').offset();
    contentOffset     = Math.round(contentOffset.left);

    var contentWidth  = $('.content-section').width();
    var contentRight  = contentOffset + contentWidth;

    _.each(imagesToAlign, function(image) {
      alignStoryImage(image, contentRight);
    });

    return false;
  };

  function alignStoryImage(image, contentRight) {
    var imageOffset, imageWidth, imageRight, newOffset,
    imagePadding, $image;

    $image = $(image);
    imageOffset  = $image.offset();
    imageOffset  = Math.round(imageOffset.left);
    imagePadding = $image.outerWidth() - $image.width();
    imageWidth   = $image.width();
    imageRight   = imageOffset + imageWidth + imagePadding;

    // there was some rounding involved, so no biggie if we're off by 1
    if(imageRight > contentRight + 1 || imageRight < contentRight - 1) {
      newOffset = contentRight - imageWidth + imagePadding;
      $image.offset({left: newOffset});
      $image.css({'margin-left': parseInt($image.css('left')) * -1});
    }

    return false;
  };

});
</script>
