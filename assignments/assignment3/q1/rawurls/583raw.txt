            <div class="story_link_container"><span class="story_headline">Getting to Know Geneva Anderson</span></div>
            <div class="story_subheadline"></div>
            <div class="story_byline"></div>
            <div class="story_date">2/10/2016 5:13:00 PM</div>     
            <div class="noprint story_email">
                <a href="http://www.addthis.com/bookmark.php?v=250&pub=xa-4a7320e7645958d0" onmouseover="return addthis_open(this, '', '[URL]', '[TITLE]')" onmouseout="addthis_close()" onclick="return addthis_sendto()"><img src="http://s7.addthis.com/static/btn/lg-share-en.gif" width="125" height="16" alt="Bookmark and Share" style="border:0"/></a><script type="text/javascript" src="http://s7.addthis.com/js/250/addthis_widget.js?pub=xa-4a7320e7645958d0"></script>

			<div class="story_content">
				 <strong>EDINBURG </strong>– University of Texas Rio Grande Valley track and field senior <dfn><a href="/roster.aspx?rp_id=4959" rel="smarttag" rev="4959">Geneva Anderson</a></dfn> loves to compete in the hurdles but it wasn't always that way.<br> <br>&quot;My freshman year, I went out in conference and I thought I was going to do ok,&quot; Anderson said. &quot;Well I fell over the first hurdle and that broke me down. I was very skeptical about running the hurdles the following year.&quot;<br> <br>Anderson used that moment in her career to grow as an athlete. She knew that she had to have faith in her training and to have the confidence to be the best at the hurdles.<br> <br>&quot;Honestly, I fell in love with the hurdles,&quot; Anderson laughed. &quot;I don't know why. I came back to get third at conference my sophomore year. Last year, I had a great year when I finished second. This year I am hoping, God willing, that I win conference and go further than conference.&quot;<br> <br>Anderson didn't start working with the hurdles until she was a sophomore in high school. Although it was only during summer track, she didn't start competing in the hurdles until her senior year.<br> <br>Track has always been a part of Anderson's life but she did play basketball and even tried some flag football. She quickly realized that her passion was for track.<br> <br>&quot;My passion for track was more than my passion for basketball,&quot; Anderson said. &quot;I loved track more than anything.&quot;<br> <br>Anderson was born in Austin but was raised in Washington, D.C. where she competed with one of the best Track AAU teams. Anderson loved the fact that track took her to different places like Florida, California and even back to Texas.<br> <br>Anderson moved to Washington because her mom wanted her to get a taste of a different environment but before entering high school it was her mom who believed she would be better recruited in Texas.<br> <br>&quot;My mom wanted me to get a taste of a different environment,&quot; Anderson said. &quot;She also felt that I could get recruited better in Texas instead of D.C. so we moved back to Texas.&quot;<br> <br>Anderson made the move back to Texas before enrolling at Round Rock High School where she lettered in basketball and track and field for four years. She thrived in both sports as she earned Defensive Player of the Year and First Team All-District as a senior, All-District Honorable Mention as a junior, All-District as a sophomore and Freshman Player of the Year as a freshman.<br> <br>On the track, she was named Best Female Athlete and earned All-District 16-5A as a senior after winning championships in the 300-meter hurdles, the 4x200-meter relay and the 4x400-meter relay.<br> <br>She also was a regional finalist as a junior, regional qualifier as a sophomore and was named the Most Outstanding Newcomer as a freshman.<br> <br>Coming out of high school, Anderson was happy to come to the Rio Grande Valley. There was one big reason why she chose the University of Texas-Pan American and that was because of Vaqueros head coach <dfn><a href="/coaches.aspx?rc=1054" rel="smarttag" rev="1054">Xavier Richardson</a></dfn>.<br> <br>&quot;Coach X came to my practice either my sophomore or junior year,&quot; Anderson said. &quot;He came to one of my summer practices and he watched me die out there. I met him then and we have had a great relationship since then. He always checked up on me and it made me feel more at home. When I finally signed here, I was very happy.&quot;<br> <br>Anderson isn't the first student-athlete to say that Coach Richardson is the reason they chose to be here. Anderson points to the way he genuinely cares about the members on his team and how he wants them to be the best that they can be.<br> <br>&quot;He wants us to have faith in him and to trust the process of becoming a better athlete,&quot; Anderson said. &quot;That is how my relationship with him grew. I have trusted him for years and this year has been one of my best so far.&quot;<br> <br>Anderson likes the fact that Coach Richardson always pushes her to be better. Coach Richardson knows where they are coming from and Anderson likes that he can relate to their situations.<br> <br>&quot;He makes sure I am on top of my stuff,&quot; Anderson said. &quot;He is very understandable, so when I talk to him, I can talk to him about anything. He understands and he gives great advice.&quot;<br> <br>All the advice she has taken over her four years at UTRGV has allowed her to grow as a person. Early on in her collegiate career, Anderson made some bad choices but she did what most college students do and that is learn from her mistakes.<br> <br>&quot;My freshman year was my toughest year,&quot; Anderson said. &quot;I got into some trouble by hanging out with the wrong people. I started to grow and learn though and finally I started to build on whom I was. I started maturing and now in my senior year I feel even more mature.&quot;<br> <br>Looking back to her time here, Anderson realizes that she must be a role model for her teammates. She knows how difficult it can be for a student-athlete and she tries to lead by example.<br> <br>&quot;I know I have people looking up to me and depending on me,&quot; Anderson said. &quot;It is always important to stay positive and keep encouraging everybody. I just try to encourage everyone to do good even when someone is feeling down. I just try to help others.&quot;<br> <br>On the track, Anderson has grown as well. After her performance at the conference meet her freshman season, Anderson bounced back with a third place finish in the 400-meter hurdles as a sophomore.<br> <br>She then improved her standing as a junior after finishing in second place in the 400-meter hurdles at the WAC Outdoor Championships where her and her teammates finished in first, second and third to dominate the podium.<br> <br>That moment where she and her teammates, <dfn><a href="/roster.aspx?rp_id=4487" rel="smarttag" rev="4487">Deandra Barroso</a></dfn> and <dfn><a href="/roster.aspx?rp_id=4988" rel="smarttag" rev="4988">Stephanie Vazquez</a></dfn>, stood on the podium has been Anderson's favorite moment of her collegiate career, so far.<br> <br>&quot;My favorite memory came last year when we took top three in the hurdles,&quot; Anderson said. &quot;That was the best. I had never seen coach so happy. That was our dream since our freshman year and we got it.&quot;<br> <br>Anderson hopes to repeat that feat this year. With Barroso now graduated it will be up to Anderson and Vazquez to hold up their end. Anderson believes it can happen again this year.<br> <br>&quot;We have three good hurdlers,&quot; Anderson said. &quot;We are running with each other and we are trusting in the process. We trust in coach and we trust in the workouts. When it comes down to it, I believe we can do it again this year.&quot;<br> <br>Anderson is majoring in criminal justice with a minor in legal studies. She plans on graduating in August and maybe one-day work with a sports agency or in sports management.<br> <br>Anderson is willing to go wherever she can get a good job at, even if it is outside of Texas. She wouldn't mind going back to the east coast.<br> <br>Once the time comes where she will have to leave UTRGV, Anderson is going to miss being able to bond with her teammates, traveling with them and of course the running.<br> <br>&quot;I am going to miss running,&quot; Anderson laughed. &quot;I am going to miss the traveling and having the team bonding. I am going to miss waking up every morning and going to weights, going to class and then going to practice. I am going to miss it all.&quot;<br><br><a href="https://give.utrgv.edu/DonateNow">Support UTRGV Track and Field</a> | <a href="https://www.facebook.com/UTRGVAthletics">Become a Fan on Facebook</a> | <a href="https://twitter.com/GoUTRGV">Follow us on Twitter</a> | <a href="http://instagram.com/goutrgv/">Follow us on Instagram</a> | <a href="http://www.youtube.com/c/goutrgvathletics">Follow us on YouTube</a><br> <div class='print-friendly-story-button'><a onclick='window.open(this.href,"_blank");return false;' href='/news/2016/2/10/WTRACK_0210161834.aspx?print=true'>Print Friendly Version</a></div>
