        <div class="post-content user-defined-markup">
            <p><img itemprop="image" src="http://media1.gameinformer.com/filestorage/CommunityServer.Components.SiteFiles/imagefeed/featured/respawn/titanfall/Overwatch610.jpg" border="0" style="max-width:610px;" alt="" /></p>
<p>It appears Electronic Arts and Respawn Entertainment are thinking big with the second installment in the Titanfall series. </p>
<p>A week after <i>Forbes</i> <a target="_blank" href="http://www.gameinformer.com/b/news/archive/2016/02/07/titanfall-2-sp-tv-show.aspx">published a report</a> claiming Titanfall 2 will include a dedicated single-player campaign and a television spinoff, today McFarlane Toys <a target="_blank" href="http://mcfarlane.com/toys/brands/titanfall/">announced</a> plans for a line of Titanfall 2 themed toys. The press release says these toys are a part of the &quot;new 2016 collector box program,&quot; stand seven inches tall, and that they are slated for a winter 2016 release &quot;in conjunction with the highly anticipated launch of Titanfall 2.&quot;</p>
<p>To learn more about how Respawn designed the original Titans, watch <a target="_blank" href="http://www.gameinformer.com/b/features/archive/2013/06/28/iron-giants-titanfalls-reinvention-of-mech-combat.aspx">Ben Hanson&#39;s video</a>.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Our Take</strong><br /><i>The winter 2016 release window for these figures is interesting considering EA&#39;s previous statement only pointed to a game launch before March 2017. If Respawn accelerates development it could make for a crowded holiday 2016 shooter space, with Gears of War 4, the next Infinity Ward Call of Duty title, and EA&#39;s own Battlefield 5 all currently slated to join the fray. However, EA and Respawn will ultimately determine when the game launches, whether or not the toy line is ready.</i></p><div style="clear:both;"></div>
