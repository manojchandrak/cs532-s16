<div class="article__body" itemprop="articleBody">
    <p>A former teacher and current ASIC senior executive has been charged with abusing a 14-year-old boy in the 1980s. </p><p>Peter Cuzner, 60 of Kaleen, pleaded not guilty on Thursday to three acts of indecency against the boy in 1986.</p><figure class="media  media--photo"data-event-tracking-enabled='false' data-track-data='{"name_component":"Inline_Image"}'>
    <img alt="Peter Cuzner (middle) leaves the ACT Magistrates Court." title="" src="/content/dam/images/g/m/r/2/l/a/image.related.articleLeadwide.620x349.gmr0yp.png/1455242479205.jpg"><figcaption class="media__caption">
    Peter Cuzner (middle) leaves the ACT Magistrates Court. &nbsp;<cite>Photo: Graham Tidy</cite>
    </figcaption>

</figure><meta itemprop="image" content="http://www.canberratimes.com.au/content/dam/images/g/m/r/2/l/a/image.related.articleLeadwide.620x349.gmr0yp.png/1455242479205.jpg"/>
<p>Cuzner was a teacher at a number of schools in the ACT, including Daramalan College, although it is not yet clear where the alleged abuse occurred. </p><p>He is currently listed as the ASIC regional commissioner for the ACT, a senior executive position, but was suspended once the organisation learned of the allegations.</p><p>Cuzner was charged after a lengthy police operation prompted by the Royal Commission into Institutional Responses to Child Sexual Abuse. </p><p>ACT Policing set up Operation Attest, a team of highly experienced child abuse investigators, last year, and revealed on Monday they were bringing four people to court this week.</p><div id="adspot-300x250-pos3" class="ad " style='display:none;'>
        <small>Advertisement</small>
        <script>
                        titan.requestConditionalAd.push("adspot-300x250-pos3");
                    </script>
                </div>
<p><a href="/act-news/two-exmarist-brothers-could-attempt-to-avoid-trial-on-child-sex-abuse-charges-20160209-gmp80i.html">Three of those cases appeared in the ACT Magistrates Court on Tuesday</a>, including two against Marist brothers, Brother John William Chute, also known as Brother Kostka, and former Brother Gregory Sutton. </p><p>Those two Marist brothers were the focus of Canberra hearings of the child sexual abuse Royal Commission. </p><p>Cuzner attended court on Thursday, but said nothing as he left the courtroom. </p><p>His case has been adjourned for 10 weeks, and will come back before the ACT Magistrates Court in late April. </p><p>ASIC spokesman Matthew Abbott said the organisation was currently reviewing who would act as its new ACT regional commissioner.</p><p>"This is a police matter and we're unable to comment at this stage," Mr Abbott said. </p><p>"Peter was stood down as of the 28th of January."</p><p>"We're reviewing who will become the next ACT regional commissioner."</p><p>On Tuesday, the ACT Magistrates Court heard that Marist Brother Kostka was suffering dementia, and was currently seeking a ruling in separate NSW proceedings that he was not fit to stand trial. </p><p>Brother Kostka's lawyer indicated a similar approach would be taken in the ACT. </p><p>Brother Sutton may try to have proceedings against him permanently stayed. He is in Australia only due to an extradition that occurred in a past case, and there may be an argument that he cannot be charged with new offences while in the country on a separate extradition. </p><p>The third man who was due to appear on Tuesday did not attend court. There is no formal proof that he was served with a summons.  </p><p><strong>An earlier version of this story suggested Peter Cuzner worked at Marist College Canberra. This was incorrect. A reporter made the error. </strong></p><p> </p>
