    <p>An Israeli court convicted Israeli Prime Minister <a href="http://www.alaraby.co.uk/english/news/2016/2/7/netanyahu-to-take-legal-action-against-palestinian-israeli-mps" target="_blank">Binyamin Netanyahu's</a> wife on Wednesday for abusing a former member of her housekeeping staff.</p>
<p>The court found "an atmosphere of harmful work conditions at the [Netanyahu] residence due to the behavior of Mrs. Netanyahu and her attitude toward the workers".</p>
<p>The judge wrote in her ruling that Sara Netanyahu engaged in "irrational demands, insults, humiliation and outbursts of rage".</p>
<p>The Jerusalem labour court awarded the former employee, Meni Naftali, $42,000 in damages and court fees.</p>
<p>The Netanyahus have been widely criticised in Israel for <a href="http://www.alaraby.co.uk/english/politics/2015/3/16/racism-paranoia-and-desperation-mark-netanyahus-election-campaign" target="_blank">leading a lavish lifestyle</a> and Sara Netanyahu has faced accusations of abusive behavior toward household staff going back to the 1990s.</p>
<p>At a press conference, Naftali thanked his supporters and said, "I am sure there will be more suits" against the prime minister's wife.</p>
<p>Talking about Sara Netanyahu's behaviour, Naftali said in one instance she called him at 3am to complain that he had bought milk in a plastic storage bag instead of a carton.</p>
<p>In another case, he says she threw a vase of day-old flowers on the floor, scolding him that they were not fresh enough.</p>

<p>Naftali has also claimed that Sara Netanyahu derided his ethnicity when he ordered food for them in a hotel, implying that his Middle Eastern background was somehow uncouth.</p>
<p>Netanyahu's office had no immediate comment. It had previously rejected Naftali's allegations, calling them "outrageous".</p>
<p>Sara Netanyahu has attracted criticism since her husband's first term as prime minister in the late 1990s for allegedly squabbling with her staff and meddling in state affairs.</p>
<p>Among other things, she was accused of firing a nanny for burning a pot of soup and of throwing a pair of shoes at an assistant.</p>
<p>Since her husband returned to office in 2009, she has kept a lower profile, though she has been unable to avoid the spotlight entirely.</p>
<p>In 2010, a former housekeeper accused her of using abusive language and forcing her to shower several times a day to ensure a "sterile" environment.<br /><br />Last week, former Israeli&nbsp;<a href="http://www.alaraby.co.uk/english/news/2015/2/11/mossad-mole-thwarted-hizballah-plot-to-assassinate-olmert">Prime Minister Ehud Olmert</a>&nbsp;admitted to obstructing justice in a series of graft-linked trials.</p>
<p>Olmert <a href="http://www.alaraby.co.uk/english/news/2016/2/3/former-israeli-pm-ehud-olmert-admits-obstructing-justice" target="_blank">faces an 18-month jail sentence</a> beginning on February 15 after being convicted of bribery, in addition to separate jail time for corruption and fraud.</p>
