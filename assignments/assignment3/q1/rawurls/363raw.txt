<h2>Milo Ventimiglia, Matt Czuchry, Liza Weil and Yanic Truesdale are now all confirmed for the Gilmore Girls Netflix revival</h2>
<p>With the <strong>Gilmore Girls </strong>Netflix revival officially confirmed as of <a href="http://www.comingsoon.net/tv/news/652377-new-gilmore-girls-episodes"  target="_blank">late last month</a>, the streaming service has today issued an update that confirms the return of several key Stars Hollow faces. <a href="https://twitter.com/miloventimiglia" onclick="javascript:_gaq.push(['_trackEvent','outbound-article','http://twitter.com']);" target="_blank">Milo Ventimiglia</a> (<strong><a href="http://www.comingsoon.net/tag/gotham"  target="_blank">Gotham</a></strong>), Matt Czuchry (<strong>The Good Wife</strong>), Liza Weil (<strong>How To Get Away With Murder</strong>) and Yanic Truesdale (<strong>Rumours</strong>) are set to return to their original characters. It has also been confirmed that the <strong>Gilmore Girls</strong> Netflix revival will include four 90-minute episodes. The series title and premiere date are to be confirmed at a later date.</p>
<p>Previously announced cast members returning for the <strong>Gilmore Girls</strong> Netflix revival include Lauren Graham (<strong>Parenthood</strong>), Alexis Bledel (<strong>The Sisterhood of the Traveling Pants</strong>), Scott Patterson (<strong>Saw V</strong>), Kelly Bishop (<strong>The Good Wife</strong>), Keiko Agena (<strong>Transformers: Dark of the Moon</strong>) and Sean Gunn (<strong>Guardians of the Galaxy</strong>). Series regulars missing from the revival include Edward Herrmann, who tragically passed away earlier this year, and Melissa McCarthy, whose stardom has soared since the series went off the air.</p>
<p><strong>Gilmore Girls</strong>, which aired between 2000 and 2007, is set in Stars Hollow, a storybook Connecticut town populated by an eclectic mix of dreamers, artists and everyday folk. The multi-generational drama about family and friendship centers around Lorelai Gilmore (Lauren Graham) and her daughter, Rory (Alexis Bledel). Lorelai owns the town&#8217;s bed-and-breakfast, the Dragonfly Inn, with best friend/chef Sookie (Melissa McCarthy), and contends with weekly dinners with eccentric, well-off parents Richard (Edward Herrmann) and Emily Gilmore (Kelly Bishop), who always have something to say about their daughter&#8217;s life.</p>
<p>Amy Sherman-Palladino is the creator and executive producer. Daniel Palladino is also an executive producer. They are both writing and directing the series. <strong>Gilmore Girls</strong> is produced by Dorothy Parker Drank Here Productions in association with Warner Bros. Television.</p>
