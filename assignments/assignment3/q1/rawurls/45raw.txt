is a fleet of warships and the redesigned Nissan Armada full-size SUV revealed this evening in Chicago does a good job of living up to that name.</p><p>The 2017 Armada is built on a new, rugged body-on-frame platform, has room for eight passengers and is equipped with a new V8 engine with 390 horsepower.</p><p>The Armada's platform was derived from the platform Nissan uses for the Infiniti QX80 SUV as well as the Nissan Patrol — a hefty SUV that Nissan sells globally but not in the U.S.</p><p>The Patrol, which traces its history to 1951, has been used by the United Nations peacekeeping forces.</p><p>Richard Miller, director of product planning for Nissan in North America, said the automaker adapted the platform to make it better suited for family vacations than toiling in the Sahara.</p><p>"(The Patrol) is known as a severe, off-road vehicle," Miller said during a media briefing Wednesday afternoon in Chicago. "The Armada is not for the severe off-road driver. Hauling boats and family adventures — that's what the Armada is built for."</p><p>Miller said the ride and handling of the new Armada is better than the outgoing version and is "whisper quiet" inside.</p><p>The key differences between the 2017 Armada and the previous generation include:</p><div><p></p><ul><li>A new exterior design</li><li>A new 390-horsepower, 5.6-liter V8 engine that is mated with a new 7-speed automatic transmission.</li><li>A number of available safety and security features including: collision warning, blind spot intervention, backup collision intervention, and moving object detection.</li><li>A quieter, "library level," riding experience for passengers.</li><li>Is 1.2 inches longer.</li><li>An all-new body that provides better ride and handling.</li></ul></div><p>The 2017 Armada will go on sale this summer at a time when gas prices are likely to still be low, which bodes well for the big SUV.</p><p>While Nissan didn't release fuel economy figures for the new version, the outgoing model got a combined fuel economy of just 15 miles per gallon.</p><p>Peter Luttenbacher, manager of product planning, declined to say what the 2017 version will get but said it will be better.</p><p>The Armada is Nissan's largest and most powerful SUV. Its redesign comes on the heels of Nissan's redesigned Pathfinder, Murano and Rogue, which have all been launched over the past three years. It competes with SUVs like the Chevrolet Tahoe, GMC Yukon, Toyota Sequoia and Toyota Land Cruiser.</p><p>Miller estimates that the large SUV segment accounts for about 300,000 in annual sales in the U.S.</p><p>Last year, Nissan sold only 12,737 Armada's, or just 1.1% more than the prior year. But Miller said Nissan stopped producing the outgoing Armada about half-way through the year.</p><p>Nissan, he said, is confident its new Armada will win over a larger share of families who need the space and comfort it is offering.</p><p>Miller also declined to say how much the Armada will cost, but with all of the improvements and the investment into the new vehicle it could easily be a few thousand dollars higher than the current Armada, which starts at $38,510 — a bargain for the segment.</p><p>"We are not looking at a big jump," Miller said.
