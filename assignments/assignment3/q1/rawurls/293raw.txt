	<div class="content clear-block">
		<div class="newsbody"><p>You know buffalo have made it in America when two former vegans are selling <a href="https://epicbar.com/bar-detail/1" rel="nofollow">bison and bacon bars</a> down in Austin, Patagonia launches its own line of <a href="http://www.patagoniaprovisions.com/products/buffalo-jerky-original" rel="nofollow">buffalo jerky</a>, a <a href="http://thekitchen.com/the-kitchen-boulder/kyle-mendenhall/" rel="nofollow">chef</a> in Colorado is serving grass-fed bison tartar to celebrate the meat’s “preciousness,” and Whole Foods is hawking the stuff at $25.99 per pound (for strip steaks).</p>
<p>Of course, bison already had it made in America back when they roamed the country’s open plains by the millions, happily eating, pooping, and procreating, while — perhaps not so happily — also serving as nourishment for Native Americans. But then European settlers showed up and, together with bovine disease, pushed the population to near extinction.</p>
