<!-- e design/gallery/caption_redesign.tpl --></div><!-- /photo float --><p>This was a big one.</p>
<p>“A major earthquake rumbled through Southern California early yesterday, killing at least 35 persons, injuring hundreds and causing more than a billion dollars in damage to homes, businesses and roadways in Los Angeles and surrounding areas,” read a story on the front page of The Chronicle on Feb. 10, 1971.</p><div class="ad"><div id="adMiddle" class="ad-inner">    <!-- hmb:ap300 ap300 ad -->
    <div id="AP300" class="ad_deferrable"><script type="text/javascript">/*<![CDATA[*/ hearstPlaceAd("AP300"); /*]]>*/ </script></div>
</div></div>
<p>What became known as the Sylmar or San Fernando earthquake hit L.A. seconds after 6 a.m. with a magnitude of 6.6 in the foothills of the San Gabriel Mountains. Sixty-four were killed and more than 2,000 injured, many in health care facility collapses.</p><!-- fixed-asset perfectPixelWide --><!-- hearst/item/standalone.tpl -->
<div class="hdnce-e hdnce-item-47193"><!-- mid:freeform.47193 -->
<div class="fixed-asset perfectPixelWide ndn_embed" id="ndn-video-player-1" data-config-distributor-id="45981" data-config-width="100%" data-config-height="9/16w"></div>
</div><!-- e fixed-asset perfectPixelWide -->
<p>The photo on The Chronicle’s cover shows the destroyed San Fernando Veterans Administration Hospital, a group of structures built in 1925 and about three miles from the quake’s epicenter. Forty-seven people died there.</p>
<p>“The shock waves were so powerful that they knocked out the seismographic instruments at the California Institute of Technology in Pasadena,” the New York Times story reported.</p>
<p>Luckily, when the quake hit many people were in bed and not on freeways or near buildings that were heavily damaged. But, “a sense of danger continued to hover over the city throughout the day.”</p>
<p><strong>Top O’ the Top of the News:</strong> “A militant Chinatown group accused police of harassment and threatened reprisal ‘by any means necessary.’ Page 9.” Well, that’s certainly bold.</p>
<p><strong>Top O’ the Top of the News, Sporting Green edition:</strong> “The San Francisco Warriors will play tonight’s game against the Seattle Super Sonics at the Cow Palace. Page 51.” For an NBA fan, this sentence is awesome in so many ways.</p>
