             <p>
	York Regional Police say one of their officers just happened to be outside a Vaughan gas station when three men accused of robbing it stepped outside to make their escape.</p>
<p>
	Just before midnight on Tuesday, the officer was patrolling in the area of Steeles and Kipling avenues because of several recent hold-ups in the area.</p>
<p>
	A few minutes later, police said three men entered a gas station near the intersection, allegedly demanding cash and cigarettes from a gas station employee. One of the men allegedly drew a handgun.</p>
<p>
	Police say the gas station employee suffered minor injuries.</p>
<p>
	As the three suspects exited the gas station, the patrolling officer challenged them and was able to stop two of them, placing them under arrest. The third suspect fled on foot.</p>
<p>
	A loaded revolver, cash and cigarettes were recovered.</p>
<p>
	An investigation linked the two suspects in custody to another robbery at the same gas station that occurred on Jan. 8, as well as a grocery store robbery on Hullmar Drive in Toronto that occurred on Jan. 21.</p>
<p>
	Felipe Udofe, 21, of no fixed address, has been charged with three counts of robbery with a firearm and three counts of wearing a disguise with intent.</p>
<p>
	Anthony Debattista, 18, of Toronto, faces the same six charges.</p>
<p>
	The outstanding suspect is described as a black male, standing five-feet-ten inches to six feet tall. He is approximately 20 to 25 years old. He was last seen wearing all black clothing.</p>
<p>
	Anyone with information is asked to contact police at 1-866-876-5423, ext. 6631, or Crime Stoppers at 1-800-222-8477 (TIPS).</p>
