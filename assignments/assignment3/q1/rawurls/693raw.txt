			<div id="mainArticleDiv" itemprop="articleBody">
				With <strong><a href="http://theconservativetreehouse.com/2016/02/08/fox-news-is-marco-rubios-unofficial-super-pac/">conservative blogs</a></strong> calling Fox News &quot;the unofficial Super Pac of Marco Rubio,&quot; the&nbsp;network may join the Florida senator as one of the big losers Tuesday night.<br />
<br />
Trump &ndash; who had been battling the network over its biased coverage &ndash; not only triumphed, but Fox's favored&nbsp;candidate came in a disappointing fifth place.<br />
<br />
&quot;I am shocked at Fox News,&quot; political strategist Dick Morris told <strong><a href="http://www.newsmaxtv.com/">Newsmax TV</a></strong> Tuesday night.<br />
<br />
Morris, a frequent past Fox commentator, said he had appeared on the network more than 2,100&nbsp;times and &quot;nobody ever told me what to say.&quot;<br />
<br />
But he has been &quot;appalled at the blatant bias in favor of Rubio&quot; demonstrated by the network in recent months.<br />
<br />
It seems like &quot;almost every second they are hyping him, promoting him,&quot; Morris said.<br />
<br />
Fox has come under sharp criticism for its after debate spin discussions, especially its focus groups&nbsp;that have had seemingly ordinary voters strongly favor Rubio.<br />
<br />
After the last debate in Iowa, <strong><a href="http://www.breitbart.com/2016-presidential-race/2016/02/04/national-journal-luntz-fails-to-disclose-rubio-connection/">Breitbart reported</a></strong> that &quot;pollster Frank Luntz got significant airtime to direct a post-debate focus group that unsurprisingly was very favorable to Rubio, the GOP establishment pick in the primary race.&quot;<br />
<br />
Breitbart said neither Fox nor Luntz disclosed the pollster&rsquo;s close ties with Rubio.<br />
<br />
According to the conservative online news service, Rubio had paid Luntz more than $250,000 for political work completed in Florida.<br />
<br />
Morris said Fox has been frequently giving positive air time to Rubio after reporting on Trump, to the detriment of other candidates.<br />
<br />
He said Fox has been downplaying Rubio&rsquo;s lack of experience and his problematic support for the Gang of Eight amnesty bill, which Morris said was the freshman Florida senator&rsquo;s &quot;singular accomplishment.&quot;<br />
<br />
After Rubio&rsquo;s &quot;Rick Perry moment&quot; &mdash; repeating a similar, scripted line four times within 20 minutes &mdash; Fox hosts and guests were in overdrive this past weekend and Monday defending the Senator as simply being &quot;consistent&quot; and suggesting the repetition of the lines was not unusual.<br />
<br />
As for Rubio&rsquo;s serious loss Tuesday in New Hampshire, Fox was still trumpeting Rubio.<br />
<br />
Fox&rsquo;s top gun commentator Charles Krauthammer was quick to place blame for Rubio&rsquo;s dismal showing on New Jersey Gov. Chris Christie.<br />
<br />
<strong><a href="http://insider.foxnews.com/2016/02/09/chris-christies-suicide-attack-rubio-helped-trump-win-nh">Krauthammer said</a></strong> Christie had engaged in a political &quot;hit&quot; on Rubio, and said his debate attacks were tantamount to a &quot;suicide attack&quot; that primarily benefited Donald Trump.<br />
<br />
Morris said that the &quot;rumor&quot; in political circles is that Rupert Murdoch, who controls the network&rsquo;s parent company 21st Century Fox, is backing Rubio&rsquo;s candidacy. Meanwhile Roger Ailes, the long-time chairman of the network, Morris said, has been favorably disposed toward Trump. <br />
