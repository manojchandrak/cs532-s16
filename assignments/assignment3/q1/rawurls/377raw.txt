          <h2 class="standfirst">More than 70 killed in attacks at a camp in Borno state for people displaced by Boko Haram-related violence.</h2>
          <p class="meta"><time datetime="11 Feb 2016 05:03 GMT">11 Feb 2016 05:03 GMT</time> | <span id="article-topics"><a href="/topics/organisations/boko-haram.html" data-topic-name="Boko Haram">Boko Haram</a>, <a href="/topics/country/nigeria.html" data-topic-name="Nigeria">Nigeria</a>, <a href="/topics/categories/war-conflict.html" data-topic-name="War &amp; Conflict">War &amp; Conflict</a>, <a href="/topics/regions/africa.html" data-topic-name="Africa">Africa</a></span></p>
        </div></hgroup></header><div id="Article-Top-Toolbar" class="ArticleTopToolbar"></div>
    <div id="vdoContainer" class="video-container"></div><script type="text/javascript">RenderPagesVideo('4749080215001', 0, '100%', 473, 'vdoContainer');</script><div id="article-body" class="article-body"><p>More than 70 people have been killed in a double suicide attack at a camp for internally displaced people in northeast Nigeria, local officials told Al Jazeera.</p>
<p>The twin suicide bombings in Borno state on Tuesday morning - reportedly committed by women - also left 78 people injured, officials added.</p>
<p>Information about the attacks was first reported by officials on Wednesday night.</p>
<div data-embed-type="Brightcove" data-embed-id="4749080215001">The bombings occurred at a camp in the town of Dikwa, some 80km northeast of Maiduguri, the birthplace of Boko Haram.</div>
<p>Ahmed Satomi, of the State Management Agency, told Al Jazeera officials had arrested one woman who had refused to detonate a bomb after travelling to the camp from a nearby town with two other bombers.</p>
<p>"The one they arrested alive, she confessed. She feel that her parents would come and that's why she refused to detonate her own bomb," Satomi said.</p>
<p>Al Jazeera's Ahmed Idris, reporting from Katsina in northern Nigeria, said attacks on civilian targets by suspected Boko Haram fighters have been on the increase recently in Nigeria.</p>
<p>"Officials are telling us that the injured have been evacuated for medical attention. The army, which is fighting Boko Haram, has yet to comment on this recent attack. But the finger of blame is already pointing at Boko Haram," Idris said.</p>
<p>The camp is home to 50,000 people driven from their homes by the armed group, whose six-year-old rebellion has killed 20,000 people and made 2.5 million homeless.</p>
<p><strong>IDP camp attacks </strong></p>
<p>Boko Haram, which has increasingly used suicide and bomb attacks as Nigeria's military pushes them out of territories they once controlled, has hit camps for internally displaced people before.</p>
<p>On September 11 last year, seven people were killed when an improvised explosive device went off at the Malkohi camp, near Yola, in neighbouring Adamawa state.</p>
<p>The camp has been used to house kidnapped women and children rescued by the military.</p>
<p>On January 31, <a class="internallink" href="http://www.aljazeera.com/news/2016/01/boko-haram-blast-kills-scores-nigeria-maiduguri-160131140615844.html" target="_blank"> at least 86 people were killed </a> in Dalori, some 12km from Maiduguri, when Boko Haram fighters attacked and burned down surrounding villages.</p>
