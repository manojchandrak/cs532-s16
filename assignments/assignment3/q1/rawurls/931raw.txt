                    <div class="intro">
          Joshua James, 24, has been&nbsp;charged with assault with a deadly weapon&nbsp;
        </div>
                  <ul class="caption meta inline-pipes-list">
                  <li class="author" itemprop="author" itemscope itemtype="http://schema.org/Person">
            <span itemprop="name"><a href="/author/matt-payton" title="Matt Payton">Matt Payton</a></span>

            <meta itemprop="representativeOfPage" content="true">
                      <!-- scald=4213941:article_image --><img itemprop="contentUrl" src="http://static.independent.co.uk/s3fs-public/styles/story_large/public/thumbnails/image/2016/02/10/07/alligator-florida-wendys-jpg.jpg" width="620" height="349" alt="" /><!-- END scald=4213941 -->                        <figcaption itemprop="description" class="caption">

  <div class="article-wrapper clearfix">
    <div class="main-content-column">
      <div class="text-wrapper" itemprop="articleBody">
        <p>A man who threw an alligator through a Wendy's drive-thru window has been charged with aggravated assault with a deadly weapon.</p>

<p>Joshua James, 24, threw the 3ft 6 ins predator through the window at the fast food restaurant in West Palm Beach, Florida.</p>

<p>He was also charged with illegally possessing an alligator and petty theft. Jail records show he was released on $6,000 bail on Tuesday.</p>

<p>James told <a href="http://www.wptv.com/news/region-c-palm-beach-county/loxahatchee-acreage/man-accused-of-tossing-gator-into-wendys-drive-thru-window" target="_blank">WPTV</a> that he was sober when he caught the alligator and threw it through the window.</p>

<p>He added:"I got into a lot trouble doing this and I definitely don't recommend doing this.</p>

<p>"Like I said this is probably the last time I do this."</p>

<div class="dnd-widget-wrapper context-sdl_editor_representation type-image"><div class="dnd-atom-rendered"><!-- scald=4213946:sdl_editor_representation --><div class="image"><img src="http://static.independent.co.uk/s3fs-public/styles/story_medium/public/thumbnails/image/2016/02/10/07/Joshua-James-florida-alligator.jpg" width="534" height="712" alt="Joshua-James-florida-alligator.jpg" title="Joshua-James-florida-alligator.jpg" /></div><!-- END scald=4213946 --></div><div class="dnd-caption-wrapper">Joshua James, 24</div></div>

<p>James' mother, Linda James described her son's actions as a "stupid prank."</p>

<div class="relatedlinkslist inline-block-related-list inline-block-left">

	<li><a href="/news/world/americas/spots-the-rare-white-alligator-has-died-in-louisiana-aged-28-10490541.html">'Spots' the rare white alligator has died in Louisiana aged 28</a></li>

</ul></div>
</div>

<p>Ms James said her son had "no problem turning himself in" and "he does stuff like this because he thinks it's funny".</p>

<p>Retired University of Florida zoologist James P. Ross said a bite from a relatively small alligator would be "comparable to a dog's".</p>

<p>Dr Ross said: "The alligator would be unlikely to 'attack' people and more likely to be in a highly traumatized and frightened defensive mode.</p>

<p>"It could, and quite likely would, snap and lunge at anyone approaching it closely and could project its head and jaws 12 to 18 inches in any direction."</p>
