Syria conflict: Rebels 'feel abandoned' by Britain and US

   10 February 2016 Last updated at 23:31 GMT

   Some 50,000 people have fled the recent fighting in northern Syria,
   according to the Red Cross who say there is an urgent need for
   deliveries of food, water and medicine.

   Backed by Russian airstrikes Syrian government forces are gaining
   ground near Aleppo with only a few towns remaining in rebel hands.

   A senior rebel commander inside Aleppo says his fighters felt abandoned
   by countries such as Britain and the United States.

   The BBC's Quentin Sommerville reports.
