   Made in Italy: Angelo Ogbonna scores first West Ham goal
   ...
   ...
   ...

   ‘Made in Italy’ is a mark of quality all over the world, nowhere more
   than on the football pitch. Let’s take a look at the Italian players
   who were in action abroad over last week.

   Angelo Ogbonna scored a decisive goal last night in the FA Cup replay
   between West Ham and Liverpool. The Azzurri defender settled the 2-1
   win that saw the Hammers through to the last sixteen. Ogbonna had only
   scored once in his career previously, nearly six years ago for Torino.

   Ogbonna hasn’t been playing many games for Bilic side in recent weeks
   and his goal could help him regain his manager’s confidence. With a few
   seconds left to play, the Italy centre back headed home from a few
   yards to score a goal that West Ham fans will not forget in a hurry.

   Staying in England, Graziano Pellè put in a solid performance at the
   weekend against West Ham. The Azzurri striker played 72 minutes with
   determination to keep alive the Saints’ dream of UEFA Europa League
   qualification.

   Giuseppe Rossi took to the pitch with Levante against Barcelona.
   Although the Blaugrana eventually won 2-0, the Italy forward put in a
   convincing performance, displaying his qualities on several occasions
   and showing he is on his way to full physical recovery.

   On the other hand, Marco Verratti could not take part in PSG’s 2-1
   victory at Marseille due to osteitis pubis. After their away win on
   Sunday night, Laurent Blanc’s side now have a 24-point lead over
   second-placed Monaco and are almost certain to be League 1 champions
   for the fourth consecutive time.
