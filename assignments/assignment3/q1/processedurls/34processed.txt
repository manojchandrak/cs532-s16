   Brentwood Councilman Steve Barr joins race for county supervisor

      By Nate Gartrell

      [14]ngartrell@bayareanewsgroup.com
      Posted:   02/10/2016 04:17:13 PM PST
      Updated:   02/11/2016 09:52:02 AM PST

      BRENTWOOD -- The already crowded race to win outgoing Contra Costa
      County Supervisor Mary Piepho's seat just attracted its sixth
      candidate.

      Brentwood Councilman Steve Barr announced Wednesday afternoon that he
      was throwing his name in the hat to be the next East Contra Costa
      Supervisor. Piepho announced last year that she would not be seeking
      re-election.

      Barr first joined the Council in 2010, and ran uncontested for
      re-election in 2014. Before that, he served on the Liberty Union High
      School District Board, and he has served on the East Contra Costa Fire
      District board of directors since 2011.

      "I feel it is the right time to for me use the experience I have gained
      serving the Brentwood residents for the last five years on city council
      and make a positive contribution to the board of supervisors on behalf
      of District 3 residents," Barr wrote in a statement to the media.

      Barr now joins East County NAACP President Odessa LeFrancois, Oakley
      Councilman Doug Hardcastle, Antioch Mayor Wade Harper, Ward 7 Director
      on the East Bay Regional Park District Board Diane Burgis, and Antioch
      Councilwoman Monica Wilson. Earlier in the week, Brentwood Mayor Bob
      Taylor made news by announcing he would not be seeking Piepho's seat.
