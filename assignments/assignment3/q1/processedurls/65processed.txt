China "next logical step" for Coca-Cola Co re-franchising programme - CEO

   By [4]Lucy Britner | 10 February 2016

   Font size [5]Email [6]Print
   The Coca-Cola Co also intends to re-franchise 100% of its US bottlers
   by the end of 2017

   The Coca-Cola Co also intends to re-franchise 100% of its US bottlers
   by the end of 2017

   The CEO of The [7]Coca-Cola Co has said the company's bottling system
   in China has evolved to the point where re-franchising is the "next
   logical step".

   Speaking to analysts following the company's [8]full-year and Q4
   results announcement yesterday, Muhtar Kent said that Coca-Cola's
   bottling partners in the country were "ready to take on additional
   territory".

   The group [9]also announced yesterday that it intends to re-franchise
   100% of its North America bottling territories by the end of 2017,
   including all cold-fill production facilities. At the same time, the
   company said it had entered into a non-binding letter of intent to
   refranchise company-owned bottling operations in China, to existing
   partners China Foods, which is part of COFCO, and Swire Beverage
   Holdings.

   Kent said the announcements, along with the pending creation of
   [10]Coca-Cola European Partners and [11]Coca-Cola Beverages Africa, as
   well as the company's investment in its Indonesian bottler, would
   strengthen the global bottling system.

   "To put it in perspective, adjusting for these transactions, the
   percent of our 2015 volumes sold through company-owned bottlers would
   have decreased from 18% to 3%," he said.

   China saw a 1% volume increase for the company in 2015, with president
   & COO James Quincey describing the environment in the country as
   "slowing".

   "We're looking to do better in 2016," he added.
