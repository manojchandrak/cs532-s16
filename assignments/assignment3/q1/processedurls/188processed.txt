Funeral of tragic Cork brothers

    08 Sep 2014

   The parents of three brothers killed in an apparent murder-suicide in
   Co Cork are appealing for more support to be offered to young men who
   have mental health difficulties.
     *
     *

   Share​ Share​ [14]Share​

   Story by UTV Staff, Belfast

   Hundreds of people turned out on Monday to pay their final respects to
   nine-year-old twins Thomas and Paddy O'Driscoll, who died after being
   fatally stabbed by their brother Jonathan, 21, last week at their home
   near Charleville.Their older sibling's body was found a short time
   later in Buttevant, about 10 miles away from the family's Deerpark
   home.CHAR(13) + CHAR(10) CHAR(13) + CHAR(10)The alarm was raised on
   Thursday evening when a member of the family arrived home and
   discovered the youngsters in the detached, pink and white bungalow just
   off the main Cork-Limerick road.The children were pronounced dead at
   the scene. A major police search was then launched for Jonathan, who
   was found dead in a forested area near a river at Buttevant.Speaking at
   the funeral mass for the three brothers, Fr Tom Naughton, from Holy
   Cross Church in Charleville, said: "The twins were loveable rogues.
   They were fun loving and energetic."He also said that the day Jonathan
   came into [adopted parents] Helen and Thomas's lives "was the happiest
   day of their lives."The twins' white coffins were placed at either side
   of their brother's in the chapel for the mass. Floral tributes, a
   horseshoe, racing car and hurling sticks were placed beside the
   coffins.Concelebrating the service was Bishop of Cloyne, William Crean
   and Father Eamon O'Brien, who instructed the twins for their First Holy
   Communion in May.Fr Naughton said that it's important for people to
   speak about what is troubling them."If these days have taught us
   anything at all, the challenge is to continue and to increase our
   efforts to understand and to assist, especially young males, to
   communicate the darkness that seems to be increasing young minds and
   hearts."The twins were buried in the Holy Cross graveyard while
   Jonathan was due to be buried later on Monday in the nearby parish of
   Kilmallock, where his maternal grandmother's grave lies.
