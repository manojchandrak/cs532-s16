   This week
   The Point Loma baseball team will host Cal State LA in its 2016 home
   opener on Thursday, Feb. 11 at 2:00 p.m. The teams will also play a
   game on Friday, Feb. 12 at PLNU, at 2:00 p.m., before closing the
   series out with a doubleheader at CSULA on Saturday starting at 11:00
   a.m.

   Follow along
   If you are unable to attend this week's games, or any PacWest games
   this season, they will all be available for fans to follow on live
   stats and live video. Live stats will be free of charge while video
   stream will vary in price based on the venue. PLNU home matches are
   $8.95 on a pay-per-view basis. To access both live stats and live video
   visit [163]plnusealions.com/gameday.

   Fiesta Fridays return
   For the second consecutive year, Point Loma will be holding a Fiesta
   Friday celebration at all its Friday games. This season the Sea Lions
   have teamed up with Miguel's Cocina to provide chips, salsa and their
   famous white sauce at every Fiesta Friday. We look forward to seeing
   everyone there.

   A look at the Sea Lions
   Point Loma opened the season with a daunting road trip to Chico State,
   and dropped all three games in the series. The Sea Lions' pitchers
   threw well, posting a 2.10 earned run average, but unfortunately the
   offense averaged only 1.0 runs per game against a quality Wildcats
   pitching staff. [164]Devin Carter went 6-for-11 over the weekend with
   two stolen bases. He is hitting .545 and leads the team with six total
   bases. Newcomer [165]Mike Gordner is hitting .308 and [166]Tanner Handa
   is batting .300. Starting pitchers [167]Kevin Lillicrop and [168]Trevor
   Abshire have 1.50 earned run averages after posting six inning starts.

   A look at the Golden Eagles
   Cal State LA opened up the season with a three-game series against
   California Baptist. CSULA went 1-2 in the series, winning the opening
   game 8-2. The Golden Eagles posted a 2.25 earned run average as staff
   and allowed just 26 hits in 28 innings. Offensively, CSULA hit .218
   while averaging 4.0 runs scored.

   Huntley named preseason All-American
   Point Loma's [169]Coleman Huntley III was named the closer on the Hero
   Sports preseason All-American team. He is the first to ever earn this
   honor. Nicknamed the "Polar Express," Huntley paced NCAA Division II
   during the regular season with 13 saves. As a junior he struck out 30
   batters in 25 innings, and ended the year with a 2-2 record and a 2.52
   ERA.

   Point Loma places two on PacWest preseason team
   [170]AJ Derr and [171]Coleman Huntley III were both named to the
   PacWest preseason team.  [172]AJ Derr, a senior from Turlock, Calif.
   (Pitman HS), earned third team All-PacWest honors last season as an
   outfielder after leading PLNU with a .340 batting average and a .434
   on-base percentage. He had 19 RBI and scored 36 runs. He also added
   nine stolen bases. [173]Coleman Huntley III paced NCAA Division II in
   the regular season with 13 saves and was named to the Daktronics
   All-West Region team.

   PLNU picked fourth in PacWest
   The Point Loma baseball team was picked to finish fourth in the PacWest
   Conference standings, according to the preseason coaches' poll.  PLNU
   is coming off a 33-18 season in 2015, in which they set the school
   record with a 17-game winning streak and qualified for the NCAA West
   Regional. The Sea Lions earned 66 points in the poll. They were three
   ahead of Fresno Pacific (63) for fourth place, and 12 back of Azusa
   Pacific (78) for third. California Baptist and Dixie State tied for
   first in the poll with 88 points apiece. CBU received six of the 10
   first-place votes from the league's coaches.

   Next week
   Point Loma will host a four-game series against Eastern New Mexico
   starting on Thursday, Feb. 18 at 2:00 p.m.
