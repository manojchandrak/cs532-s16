   Health Secretary Jeremy Hunt leaves Downing Street

   Junior doctor Rachel Clarke wants an interview with Jeremy Hunt (but
   she isn't going to get one)
   Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

   As the conflict between doctors and Health Secretary Jeremy Hunt
   continues, one junior doctor has grabbed the headlines with a
   stunningly eloquent interview on BBC News.
   PREVIOUS ARTICLE
   Blue Peter presenter to Zorb across the Irish Channel
   NEXT ARTICLE
   The X-Files scores huge ratings for Channel 5

   Yesterday (February 8) on The Andrew Marr Show, Hunt was read letters
   by junior doctors, including one from Dr Rachel Clarke.

   Hunt claimed that the reason for the conflict was the British Medical
   Association (BMA) union, who he called "totally irresponsible".

   Speaking later on BBC News, Clarke said: "It's extraordinary for me as
   a frontline Junior Doctor to hear my Health Secretary say that."

   Peter Capadi supports Wear Your NHS
   Related Article
   The junior doctors have not one but TWO Time Lords on their side as
   Peter Capaldi and John Hurt lend support

   She added that she hoped he would take the concerns of frontline staff
   seriously, but instead "he seemed there to have used my concerns as an
   opportunity to score a cheap political point at the expense of the
   BMA".

   Clarke added: "He says he cares about junior doctors' morale, so I can
   tell him now that the single biggest problem for my morale - the thing
   that is making me want to quit my profession at the moment - is not the
   BMA.

   "It's nothing I hear from my union or read in the press. It is what he,
   my Health Secretary, says.

   "It is the way he spins against us, he manipulates statistics against
   us and frankly he lies. One striking example being he claims to the
   media we would receive an 11% pay rise in his contract

   "It sounded very slick, it was nice spin for the media. Every junior
   doctor in the country looked at the detail in his contract and saw that
   alleged pay rise was offset by stringent cuts in our pay elsewhere."

   Pushed on the matter of Hunt's honesty, she added: "I'm saying he's
   lying."

   #JuniorDoctorsStrike was a leading trend throughout the day, and

   Clarke went on to write a column for [227]The Independent urging Hunt
   to engage with junior doctors and resolve the conflict.

   "We are sick and tired of being spun against because trust, more than
   anything, is our profession's core value," she said.
